##  Practice Exercises Part

### Exercise 1: Choose the Correct Verb (Base Form)

1. Please __________ the blanket over the bed.
2. I just want to __________ down and relax.
3. Chickens __________ eggs, but ducks do too.
4. The dog likes to __________ at my feet.
5. __________ the documents on my desk before you leave.

### Exercise 5: Correct the Mistakes
*Find and correct the error in each sentence.*

1. I was so tired that I laid down for an hour.
2. Please lie the map on the table so we can all see it.
3. She has layed the table for dinner.
4. The dog has laid in the same spot all morning.
5. He is lieing to me about his whereabouts. (Hint: This is the "untruth" meaning).

## Part 7: Answer Key

**Exercise 1**
1. **lay** (Object = *blanket*)
2. **lie** (No object; reclining)
3. **lay** (Object = *eggs*)
4. **lie** (No object; reclining)
5. **Lay** (Object = *documents*)

**Exercise 2**
1. **lay** (Past of *lie* = reclined)
2. **laid** (Past of *lay* = placed an object)
3. **lain** (Past participle of *lie* = reclined)
4. **laid** (Past of *lay* = placed an object)
5. **lain** (Past participle of *lie* = reclined)

**Exercise 3**
1. **lying** (Reclining)
2. **laying** (Placing/producing an object—*egg*)
3. **lying** (Reclining)
4. **laying** (Placing an object—*baby*)
5. **lying** (Reclining/being in a flat position)

**Exercise 4**
1. **laid** (Past of *lay*—object = *jacket*)
2. **lain** (Past participle of *lie*—reclined)
3. **lay** (Present base of *lay*—object = *shoes*)
4. **lying** (Present participle of *lie*—reclining)
5. **lain** (Past participle of *lie*—reclining)
6. **laying** (Present participle of *lay*—object = *foundation*)
7. **lay** (Past of *lie*—reclined)
8. **lay** (Present base of *lay*—object = *keys*)

**Exercise 5**
1. I was so tired that I **lay** down for an hour. (*Laid* is for objects; here it's reclining).
2. Please **lay** the map on the table. (*Lie* cannot take an object; *lay* means "place").
3. She has **laid** the table for dinner. (*Layed* is not a word; the past participle of *lay* is *laid*).
4. The dog has **lain** in the same spot all morning. (*Laid* means "placed"; the dog is reclining, so use *lain*).
5. He is **lying** to me about his whereabouts. (For "telling an untruth," the -ing form is *lying*, not *lieing*).
