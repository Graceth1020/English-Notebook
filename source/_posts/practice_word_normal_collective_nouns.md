#### Exercise A: Choose the correct verb.
*(Answers are at the bottom)*

1.  The football team (is / are) practicing for the championship game.
2.  The committee (has / have) finally made its decision.
3.  My family (loves / love) to go camping together every summer.
4.  The flock of birds (flies / fly) south for the winter.
5.  The audience (was / were) clapping their hands at different times.
6.  The staff (works / work) very hard to meet the deadline.
7.  A swarm of bees (is / are) flying towards the garden.
8.  The class (has / have) finished their individual projects.

---

#### Exercise B: Rewrite the sentences correctly.
*Some are correct; some are wrong. Fix the wrong ones.*

1.  The crowd are cheering loudly for the winner. *(Hint: They are cheering together)*
2.  The jury have reached a unanimous verdict.
3.  The pack of wolves hunts together at night.
4.  The family are sitting in different rooms watching different shows.
5.  The fleet of ships sails out to sea tomorrow.

#### Exercise C: Fill in the blanks with the correct verb (Singular or Plural).

1.  The orchestra __________ (play/plays) beautifully as one unit.
2.  The orchestra __________ (tune/tunes) their instruments individually before the show.
3.  A bunch of grapes __________ (is/are) on the table.
4.  The couple __________ (walks/walk) hand in hand every evening.
5.  The couple __________ (argue/argues) about everything!

---

### Part 5: Answers

**Exercise A:**
1.  **is** (One team practicing together)
2.  **has** (One decision)
3.  **loves** (One family activity)
4.  **flies** (One flock traveling together)
5.  **were** (Clapping at different times = individuals)
6.  **works** (One unit meeting a deadline)
7.  **is** (One swarm)
8.  **have** ("Their individual" shows they are separate, so plural)

**Exercise B:**
1.  **Incorrect.** Correction: "The crowd **is** cheering loudly for the winner." (They are acting as one).
2.  **Incorrect.** Correction: "The jury **has** reached a unanimous verdict." (Unanimous = one decision).
3.  **Correct.** ("Hunts" is singular).
4.  **Correct.** ("Are" is plural because they are doing different things).
5.  **Correct.** ("Sails" is singular because they go together).

**Exercise C:**
1.  **plays** (Singular: they act as one beautiful unit).
2.  **tune** (Plural: each musician tunes their own instrument).
3.  **is** (Singular: "a bunch" is one item/quantity).
4.  **walks** (Singular: The couple acts as one romantic unit).
5.  **argue** (Plural: The two people fight separately).
