## ordinary version:

The headmaster's speech was given in the school hall last Tuesday. At first, he thanked all the pupils coming to the hall. He shared his past forty years experience as a teacher, when he had been patient with his pupils and kindly encouraged them whenever they went unwillingly to school. To our surprise, he remembered many pupils' past and present, no matter bad students or good students. He mentioned many successful careers of some former pupils and hoped that current students could learn something from them. Some humorous incidents were be shared too. His speech had provoked many memories between him and his pupils. He also welcomed old pupils to come and visit him. Then, he told his plan for the future. He would spend his time for gardening, a entirely hobby which he wanted to do long time ago. He would take the rest of life to make his garden in order.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| coming to the hall | "Coming" implies they were in the process of arriving; "who had come" or "present in" is better | who had come to the hall |
| past forty years experience | Missing apostrophe – "forty years' experience" (possessive plural) | forty years' experience |
| went unwillingly to school | Unnatural phrasing – "were reluctant to go to school" is better | were reluctant to go to school |
| many pupils' past and present | Vague and awkward – better to specify "past and present situations" or rephrase | many pupils' past and present circumstances |
| no matter bad students or good students | Missing "whether" – "no matter whether they were bad students or good students" | no matter whether they were bad or good students |
| were be shared | Incorrect verb form – "were shared" or "were being shared" – remove "be" | were shared |
| had provoked | Past perfect unnecessary – simple past "provoked" is fine | provoked |
| memories between him and his pupils | Wrong preposition – "memories between" is odd; "memories of" or "memories shared with" is better | memories shared with his pupils |
| spend his time for gardening | Wrong preposition – "spend time on gardening" is correct | spend his time on gardening |
| a entirely hobby | Wrong article – "entirely" starts with a vowel sound, so use "an" | an entirely new hobby |
| which he wanted to do long time ago | Missing "a" – "a long time ago" | a long time ago |
| He would take the rest of life | Missing "his" – "the rest of his life" | the rest of his life |
| to make his garden in order | Unnatural phrasing – "to put his garden in order" or "to arrange his garden" | to put his garden in order |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

The headmaster's speech was given in the school hall last Tuesday. At first, he thanked all the pupils **who had come** to the hall. He shared his past **forty years'** experience as a teacher, when he had been patient with his pupils and kindly encouraged them whenever they **were reluctant to go** to school. To our surprise, he remembered many pupils' **past and present circumstances**, **no matter whether they were bad or good students**. He mentioned many successful careers of some former pupils and hoped that current students could learn something from them. Some humorous incidents **were shared** too. His speech **provoked** many **memories shared with** his pupils. He also welcomed old pupils to come and visit him. Then, he told his plan for the future. He would spend his time **on** gardening, **an entirely new** hobby which he wanted to do **a long time ago**. He would take **the rest of his life** to **put** his garden **in order**.

He would **spend his time on gardening – a completely new** hobby **he had wanted to take up for** a long time. He would **devote** the rest of his life to **putting** his garden in order.
