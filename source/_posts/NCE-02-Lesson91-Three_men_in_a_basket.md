## ordinary version:

The police did not believe what the command officer explained and still decide to arrest them. Although the men insisted who they were, they were not believed, for they could not provide any evidence to prove their identity. Then, they were forced to take to the police station. On their way, the men became quiet and thought how to persuade the police. After they reached police station, the men were questioned but refused to answer because the police's attitude was bad. When the staff of the air force station knew what happened, one of the Commanding Officer telephoned the police station immediately. He explained the reason why the arrested men took photos in the balloon. A senior officer also arrived the police station to identify the C.O. at the same time. Finally, the men were released and the police apologized to them for their misbehavior.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| still decide | Tense error – "decide" is present; story is past tense – should be "decided" | still decided |
| to arrest them | "Them" could refer to the command officer or the men – clarify; it refers to the men | to arrest the men |
| insisted who they were | Wrong structure – "insisted on who they were" or "insisted that they were" – "insisted who" is incorrect | insisted that they were who they claimed to be |
| they were not believed, for they could not | "For" is acceptable but formal; "because" is more natural. Comma before "for" is correct | they were not believed, because they could not |
| forced to take to the police station | Missing "be" – passive voice should be "forced to be taken" | forced to be taken to the police station |
| thought how to persuade | Unnatural – "thought about how to persuade" or "wondered how to persuade" | wondered how to persuade |
| After they reached police station | Missing "the" – "reached the police station" | After they reached the police station |
| refused to answer because the police's attitude was bad | "Police's" is awkward; "the police" as a collective noun can take a possessive, but "the attitude of the police" is better | because the attitude of the police was bad |
| When the staff of the air force station knew | Tense could be improved – "learned" or "heard" is more natural | When the staff learned |
| one of the Commanding Officer | Subject-verb agreement – "one of" takes a plural noun, so "Commanding Officers" | one of the Commanding Officers |
| the reason why the arrested men took photos in the balloon | "Reason why" is redundant – "reason" alone is sufficient; also "in the balloon" should be "from the balloon" (they were taking photos *of* or *from* the balloon) | the reason the arrested men took photos from the balloon |
| arrived the police station | Missing preposition – "arrived at" | arrived at the police station |
| identify the C.O. | "C.O." is introduced here but not explained – it's "Commanding Officer" – but since it was already mentioned, it's acceptable | identify the C.O. |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

The police did not believe what the command officer explained and still **decided** to arrest **the men**. Although the men **insisted that they were who they claimed to be**, they were not believed, **because** they could not provide any evidence to prove their identity. Then, they were forced to **be taken** to the police station. On their way, the men became quiet and **wondered** how to persuade the police. After they reached **the** police station, the men were questioned but refused to answer **because the attitude of the police was bad**. When the **staff learned** what happened, **one of the Commanding Officers** telephoned the police station immediately. He explained the **reason** the arrested men took photos **from** the balloon. A senior officer also **arrived at** the police station to identify the C.O. at the same time. Finally, the men were released and the police apologized to them for their misbehavior.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

The police did not believe **the** command officer's explanation and **decided** to arrest the men. Although **the men insisted on their identity**, they **were not trusted because** they could not provide any evidence to prove **who they were**. Then, **they were taken** to the police station. **On the way**, the men **fell silent and** wondered how to **convince** the police. **At** the police station, **they** were questioned but refused to **cooperate**, **as** the police **were rude**. When **the air force staff learned** what **had** happened, **one** of the Commanding Officers **called** the police station immediately **and** explained **that** the arrested men had taken photos **from** the balloon. A senior officer also **went to** the police station to **confirm** the C.O.'s identity at the same time. Finally, the men **were set free**, and the police apologized **for their mistake**.
