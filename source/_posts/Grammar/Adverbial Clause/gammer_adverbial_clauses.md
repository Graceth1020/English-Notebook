An **adverbial clause** is a group of words with a subject and a verb that acts like an **adverb**—it modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb by answering questions like *when, where, why, how, under what condition*, etc.

---

## The 9 Types of Adverbial Clauses

Each type is introduced by a **subordinating conjunction** (a "trigger word"). Here they are, organized by function:

---

### 1. Time Clauses (时间状语从句)

**Answer:** *When?*

**Common conjunctions:** when, while, as, after, before, since, until/till, as soon as, once, whenever, by the time

**Examples:**
- *I will call you **when I arrive**.*
- *She stayed there **until he came back**.*
- **After he finished work**, he went home.

⚠️ **Important:** In time clauses about the future, use **present tense**, not "will":
- ✅ *I'll tell him **when he comes**.*
- ❌ *I'll tell him when he will come.*

---

### 2. Place Clauses (地点状语从句)

**Answer:** *Where?*

**Common conjunctions:** where, wherever, everywhere, anywhere

**Examples:**
- *Sit **wherever you like**.*
- *He followed her **wherever she went**.*
- *I'll find you **where you are**.*

---

### 3. Reason Clauses (原因状语从句)

**Answer:** *Why?*

**Common conjunctions:** because, since, as, now that, seeing that

**Examples:**
- *I stayed home **because it was raining**.*
- **Since you're here**, let's start.
- *She passed the exam **as she studied hard**.*

**Nuance:**
- **Because** = strongest, direct reason.
- **Since / As** = lighter, "given that" meaning (often known information).

---

### 4. Purpose Clauses (目的状语从句)

**Answer:** *For what purpose?*

**Common conjunctions:** so that, in order that, lest (formal), in case

**Examples:**
- *I left early **so that I wouldn't miss the bus**.*
- *She spoke slowly **in order that everyone could understand**.*
- *Take an umbrella **in case it rains**.*

⚠️ After *so that* / *in order that*, use:
- **can/will** (present/future possibility)
- **could/would** (past)

---

### 5. Result Clauses (结果状语从句)

**Answer:** *What happened as a consequence?*

**Common conjunctions:** so...that, such...that, so that (without comma)

**Examples:**
- *It was **so** hot **that we went swimming**.*
- *She was **such** a good teacher **that everyone loved her**.*
- *He ran fast **so that he caught the train**.* (Here it means *with the result that*)

**Pattern:**
- **so + adjective/adverb + that**
- **such + (adjective) + noun + that**

---

### 6. Condition Clauses (条件状语从句)

**Answer:** *Under what condition?*

**Common conjunctions:** if, unless, provided (that), as long as, on condition that, in case, even if, whether...or not

**Examples:**
- **If it rains**, we'll cancel the trip.
- *You won't pass **unless you study**.* (= if you don't study)
- *I'll go **provided that you come too**.*

⚠️ **Zero, First, Second, Third Conditionals** all use these clauses. Also, like time clauses, use **present tense** for future conditions:
- ✅ *If she **comes**, I'll tell her.*
- ❌ *If she will come...*

---

### 7. Concession / Contrast Clauses (让步状语从句)

**Answer:** *Despite what?* (shows unexpected result)

**Common conjunctions:** although, though, even though, while, whereas, despite the fact that, regardless of whether

**Examples:**
- **Although it was late**, she kept working.
- *He went out **even though it was raining**.*
- *She likes coffee, **whereas I prefer tea**.* (direct contrast)

**Nuance:**
- **although / even though** = strong contrast
- **while / whereas** = direct comparison/contrast

---

### 8. Manner Clauses (方式状语从句)

**Answer:** *How? In what way?*

**Common conjunctions:** as, as if, as though, the way

**Examples:**
- *Do it **as I showed you**.*
- *He talks **as if he knew everything**.* (unreal situation → past tense)
- *She acted **as though nothing had happened**.*

⚠️ After *as if / as though*, use past tense for unreal/hypothetical situations:
- *He looks **as if he were** (or *was*) tired.*

---

### 9. Comparison Clauses (比较状语从句)

**Answer:** *Compared to what?*

**Common conjunctions:** than, as...as, not as...as, the more...the more, so...as

**Examples:**
- *She runs faster **than I do**.*
- *He is **as tall as his father** (is).*
- **The more you practice**, the better you become.

⚠️ These often have **ellipsis** (missing words) — *than I do* instead of *than I run*.

---

## Summary Table

| Type | Question Answered | Common Conjunctions |
|------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Time** | When? | when, after, before, until, as soon as |
| **Place** | Where? | where, wherever |
| **Reason** | Why? | because, since, as |
| **Purpose** | For what? | so that, in order that, in case |
| **Result** | What consequence? | so...that, such...that |
| **Condition** | Under what condition? | if, unless, provided that |
| **Concession** | Despite what? | although, even though, whereas |
| **Manner** | How? | as, as if, as though |
| **Comparison** | Compared to what? | than, as...as, the more...the more |

## One Important Grammar Rule (for all adverbial clauses)

If the adverbial clause comes **before** the main clause, put a **comma** after it:
> *When I arrived, she was already there.*

If it comes **after** the main clause, **no comma**:
> *She was already there when I arrived.*

## Quick Test (for yourself)

Try to identify the type of each clause:

1. *She smiled **as if she knew the secret**.*
2. *We left early **so that we could beat the traffic**.*
3. ***Although he was tired**, he finished the race.*
4. ***If you heat water**, it boils.*
5. *I'll stay **where you stay**.*

*(Answers: ① Manner, ② Purpose, ③ Concession, ④ Condition, ⑤ Place)*
