## ordinary version:

During the rescue operation, although the worker were drilling a hole carefully, there was loud noise which caused the root of the mine to collapse. The microphone had become silent since the collapse happened. People above the mine looked at each other seriously, worrying about the safety of the men. Fortunately, the men's voices were heard an hour later. They said weakly that they were all right. But the collapse had made the space tighter. After hearing that, the rescuers said the drilling would continue. They started drilling again and were surprised that the collapse had made things easier. The hard layer of stock were collapsed too. It's was lucky that the men were bought to the surface safely. At this moment, all the people outside became excited, and the men's relatives were moved to tears.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| the worker were | Subject-verb agreement – "worker" is singular, but "were" is plural; should be "workers" | the workers were |
| there was loud noise | Missing article – "there was a loud noise" (countable noun) | there was a loud noise |
| the root of the mine | Unnatural word – "root" is for plants; "roof" is correct for a mine | the roof of the mine |
| The microphone had become silent since the collapse happened | Tense issue – "had become" (past perfect) with "since" + past simple is acceptable, but "went silent" is more natural | The microphone went silent after the collapse happened |
| People above the mine | Preposition issue – "above the mine" is okay, but "at the surface" or "on top" is clearer | People at the surface |
| looked at each other seriously | "Seriously" is vague – "anxiously" or "gravely" is better | looked at each other anxiously |
| worrying about | Correct | (no change) |
| the safety of the men | Correct | (no change) |
| were heard an hour later | Correct | (no change) |
| They said weakly | Correct | (no change) |
| But the collapse had made the space tighter | "Tighter" is odd for space; "smaller" or "more cramped" is better | made the space smaller |

| were surprised that | Correct | (no change) |
| The hard layer of stock | Wrong word – "stock" means inventory; "rock" is correct | The hard layer of rock |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

During the rescue operation, although **the workers were** drilling a hole carefully, **there was a loud noise** which caused **the roof** of the mine to collapse. The microphone **went silent after** the collapse happened. People **at the surface** looked at each other **anxiously**, worrying about the safety of the men. Fortunately, the men's voices were heard an hour later. They said weakly that they were all right. But the collapse had **made the space smaller**. After hearing that, the rescuers said the drilling would continue. They started drilling again and were surprised that the collapse had made things easier. The hard layer of **rock collapsed** too. **It was** lucky that the men were **brought** to the surface safely. **At that moment**, all the people outside became excited, and the men's relatives were moved to tears.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

During the rescue operation, **the workers** were drilling carefully **when** a loud noise **caused** the roof of the mine to collapse. The microphone **went silent** after the collapse. **Those** at the surface looked at each other anxiously, worrying about the **trapped** men. Fortunately, the men's voices were heard an hour later. **They spoke weakly but said** they were all right. However, the collapse had **made their space even smaller**. **Hearing this**, the rescuers **decided** the drilling **had to** continue. They started drilling again **and were surprised to find** that the collapse had **actually made things easier** – the hard layer of rock **had collapsed too**. **It was** lucky that the men were **brought** to the surface safely. **At that moment**, everyone outside **cheered**, and the men's relatives were moved to tears.
