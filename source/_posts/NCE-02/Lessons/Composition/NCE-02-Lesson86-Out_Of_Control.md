## ordinary version:

A speedboat running on the sea was suddenly out of control. Fortunately, there was on one but the driver in it. The speedboat turned in a circle quickly so that the driver gripped the steering wheel tightly in order not to be thrown. It was moving towards a small fishing boat which was not far away from the speedboat. The fishermen had no sooner tried their best to row away immediately than they noticed the speedboat. The speedboat came nearer in a few seconds. The fishermen didn't have enough time to row away so they had to dive into the sea. The speedboat struck the fishing boat at a tremendous speed and kept moving in a circle until running out of petrol. Finally, it stopped just before reaching the fishermen who were worry about whether they would lose their life here.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| running on the sea | "On the sea" is acceptable, but "at sea" or "on the water" is more natural | running at sea |
| there was on one | Typo – "on" should be "no" | there was no one |
| but the driver in it | "But" is used correctly here (meaning "except"), but the phrasing is awkward – "except for the driver" is clearer | except for the driver |
| turned in a circle quickly | Word order – "turned quickly in a circle" or "spun in circles" is more natural | turned quickly in a circle |
| so that the driver gripped | "So that" implies purpose – the driver gripped *in order* to not be thrown, not *as a result* of the turning. Better: "so the driver gripped" or "and the driver gripped" | so the driver gripped |
| in order not to be thrown | Correct but formal – "to avoid being thrown" is more natural | to avoid being thrown |
| The fishermen had no sooner tried their best to row away immediately than they noticed the speedboat | Serious errors: (1) "no sooner... than" requires past perfect, but the structure is misused – "had no sooner done X than Y" means Y happened immediately after X. Here they noticed *before* they tried to row, so this is backwards. (2) "immediately" is redundant with "no sooner." | As soon as they noticed the speedboat, they tried their best to row away |
| The speedboat came nearer in a few seconds | "In a few seconds" implies it took a few seconds to happen; "within a few seconds" is better | within a few seconds |
| didn't have enough time | Correct | (no change) |
| so they had to dive | Correct | (no change) |
| struck the fishing boat at a tremendous speed | Preposition – "at a tremendous speed" is acceptable, but "with" or "at" both work | at a tremendous speed (acceptable) |
| kept moving in a circle | Correct | (no change) |
| until running out of petrol | Missing subject – "until it ran out of petrol" | until it ran out of petrol |
| who were worry | Wrong adjective form – "were worried" (past participle adjective) | who were worried |
| whether they would lose their life | "Life" should be plural – "their lives" | whether they would lose their lives |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

A speedboat **at sea** was suddenly out of control. Fortunately, there was **no one** **except for the driver** in it. The speedboat **turned quickly in a circle**, **so** the driver gripped the steering wheel tightly **to avoid being thrown**. It was moving towards a small fishing boat which was not far away. **As soon as** the fishermen noticed the speedboat, they tried their best to row away. The speedboat came nearer **within** a few seconds. The fishermen didn't have enough time to row away, so they had to dive into the sea. The speedboat struck the fishing boat at a tremendous speed and kept moving in a circle **until it ran out of petrol**. Finally, it stopped just before reaching the fishermen, who **were worried** about whether they would lose **their lives**.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

A speedboat **at sea** suddenly **went out of** control. Fortunately, **no one was on board except** the driver. The speedboat **spiraled** quickly, **and** the driver gripped the steering wheel tightly **to avoid being thrown overboard**. It was heading straight toward a small fishing boat **nearby**. **The moment** the fishermen noticed the speedboat, they **tried desperately** to row away. **But** the speedboat **closed in** within seconds. **With no time to escape**, the fishermen **jumped** into the sea. The speedboat **smashed into** the fishing boat at **full speed** and kept **spinning** in circles until **its fuel ran out**. Finally, it stopped just **short of** the fishermen, who **were terrified that they might lose their lives**.
