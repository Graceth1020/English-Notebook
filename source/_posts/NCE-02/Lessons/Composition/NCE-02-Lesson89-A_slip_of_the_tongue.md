## ordinary version:

U. Bird Seed company packed the local cinema to present a free comedy show yesterday. When the news got round, local residents all rushed to the cinema. There was a long queue of people outside the cinema. As the cinema's seats was limited, many people failed to get in. However, many famous actors who should have appeared did not come so the comedy was dull. People failing to watch were the lucky ones. Now let us give a description of the stage. There was a bare stage with only one microphone and a large, ugly model of a yellow bird beside it. Free packets were also prepared for the audiences. The only funny thing about the show was the advertiser. As he was too nervous to speak, he stood awkwardly on the stage. As soon as he opened his mouth, everyone burst out laughing. The audience knew what he should have said, though he kept making mistakes. The rest of the show was not as amusing as the audience had expected, so they felt somewhat disappointed.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| U. Bird Seed company | Abbreviation "U." is unclear – better to spell out "U. Bird" as a name, or add "The" before the company name | The U. Bird Seed Company |
| packed the local cinema | "Packed" here suggests the company filled the cinema, but the intended meaning is likely "filled" – but the company didn't pack it, the people did. Better: "filled the local cinema" or "attracted a full house" | filled the local cinema |
| When the news got round | Correct British expression ("got round" = spread) – acceptable, but "got around" is more common in American English | got around (or "spread") |
| local residents all rushed to the cinema | Correct | (no change) |
| the cinema's seats was limited | Subject-verb agreement – "seats" is plural, so "were" is correct | the cinema's seats were limited |
| However, many famous actors who should have appeared did not come so the comedy was dull | Missing comma before "so" – separating two independent clauses | did not come, so the comedy was dull |
| People failing to watch were the lucky ones | "Failing to watch" is awkward – better "Those who failed to get in" or "Those who didn't watch" | Those who failed to get in were the lucky ones |
| Now let us give a description of the stage | Awkward and overly formal – "Now let us describe the stage" is more concise | Now let us describe the stage |
| Free packets were also prepared for the audiences | "Audiences" is plural – should be "audience" (collective noun, singular) | for the audience |
| The only funny thing about the show was the advertiser | "Advertiser" is a person who advertises; but the context suggests a "salesman" or "promoter" – or it might refer to a person presenting the ad. "Advertiser" is acceptable but "promoter" might be better | the promoter |
| As he was too nervous to speak, he stood awkwardly on the stage | Correct | (no change) |
| As soon as he opened his mouth, everyone burst out laughing | Correct | (no change) |
| The audience knew what he should have said, though he kept making mistakes | Correct | (no change) |
| The rest of the show was not as amusing as the audience had expected, so they felt somewhat disappointed | Correct | (no change) |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

**The U. Bird Seed Company filled** the local cinema to present a free comedy show yesterday. When the news **got around**, local residents all rushed to the cinema. There was a long queue of people outside the cinema. As the cinema's seats **were** limited, many people failed to get in. However, many famous actors who should have appeared did not come **,** so the comedy was dull. **Those who failed to get in** were the lucky ones. Now let us **describe** the stage. There was a bare stage with only one microphone and a large, ugly model of a yellow bird beside it. Free packets were also prepared for **the audience**. The only funny thing about the show was **the promoter**. As he was too nervous to speak, he stood awkwardly on the stage. As soon as he opened his mouth, everyone burst out laughing. The audience knew what he should have said, though he kept making mistakes. The rest of the show was not as amusing as the audience had expected, so they felt somewhat disappointed.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

**The U. Bird Seed Company** filled the local cinema **yesterday for** a free comedy show. When the news **spread**, local residents **flocked** to the cinema. There was a long queue outside. **Since** the cinema's seats were limited, many people failed to get in. However, many famous actors who **were supposed to appear** did not come, **making** the comedy dull. **Those who didn't get in** were **actually** the lucky ones. Now **let's describe** the stage **itself** – it was bare **except for** a microphone and a large, ugly model of a yellow bird beside it. Free **gift** packets were also prepared for the audience. The only funny thing about the show was **the promoter**. He stood awkwardly on stage, **too nervous to speak**. But as soon as he opened his mouth, everyone burst out laughing. The audience knew **exactly** what he **meant to say**, though he kept making mistakes. The rest of the show was not as amusing as the audience had expected, so they felt **rather** disappointed.
