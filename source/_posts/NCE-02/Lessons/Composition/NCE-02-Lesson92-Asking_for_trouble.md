## ordinary version:

At the first my wife did not recognize me as the light was so dark. She did not wake up completely. After seeing me, she thought that I was a thief. She was terrified and called the police for help when she saw the policeman. The policeman immediately pulled me down the ladder. I felt dizzy after I was pulled down to the ground. Then, I shouted my wife's name loudly, touching my hurt back. Fortunately, my wife recognized my voice. She realized her mistake and explained to the policeman who I was. The policeman smiled to me and gave me a hand after knowing the truth. At the same time ,my wife went downstairs quickly and apologized to the policeman. The policeman also apologized to me for his rude behaviour.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| At the first | Wrong expression – "At first" is correct (without "the") | At first |
| as the light was so dark | "Light was dark" is contradictory – "light" is bright; should say "it was so dark" or "the lighting was so poor" | because it was so dark |
| She did not wake up completely | Correct, but more natural: "She had not fully woken up" | She had not fully woken up |
| After seeing me | "After seeing me" is fine, but "When she saw me" flows better | When she saw me |
| called the police for help when she saw the policeman | Illogical – she called the police *and then* saw the policeman? Or she called them *because* she saw him? Should clarify: "She was terrified and called the police. When the policeman arrived..." | She was terrified and called the police. When the policeman arrived... |
| pulled me down the ladder | "Down the ladder" is unclear – "down from the ladder" is correct | down from the ladder |
| after I was pulled down to the ground | Redundant – "down from the ladder" already implies reaching the ground | after I was pulled down (or "after landing") |
| touching my hurt back | "Hurt back" is acceptable but "aching back" or "injured back" is more natural | touching my aching back |
| smiled to me | Wrong preposition – "smiled at me" is correct | smiled at me |
| gave me a hand | Correct idiom – means "helped me" | (no change) |
| after knowing the truth | Correct, but "after learning the truth" is more natural | after learning the truth |
| At the same time ,my wife | Space before comma – remove the space | At the same time, my wife |
| apologized to the policeman | Correct | (no change) |
| apologized to me for his rude behaviour | Correct | (no change) |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

**At first**, my wife did not recognize me **because it was so dark**. **She had not fully woken up**. **When** she **saw** me, she thought that I was a thief. She was terrified and called the police for help. **When the policeman arrived**, he immediately pulled me **down from** the ladder. I felt dizzy after **landing**. Then, I shouted my wife's name loudly, touching **my aching back**. Fortunately, my wife recognized my voice. She realized her mistake and explained to the policeman who I was. The policeman **smiled at** me and gave me a hand **after learning** the truth. At the same time **,** my wife went downstairs quickly and apologized to the policeman. The policeman also apologized to me for his rude behaviour.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

At first, my wife **couldn't** recognize me because it was so dark **inside** **and** she **wasn't fully awake**. **When** she **saw** me, she thought **I was** a thief. **Terrified**, she called the police. **When the officer arrived**, he **immediately** pulled me **off** the ladder. I felt dizzy **once I was on the ground**. Then, I shouted my wife's name **as I rubbed my sore back**. **Luckily**, she recognized my voice. **She** realized her mistake and explained **to the officer who I really was**. The policeman smiled at me and **helped me up** after **hearing the truth**. **Meanwhile**, my wife came downstairs quickly and apologized to him. **The officer** also apologized to me for his **rough** handling.
