## ordinary version:

While hearing that the basement was on fire, the Ambassador went down to see what had happened. However, when his assistant Horst saw him, he aimed him a fire extinguisher and explained that he was on fire. Though the Ambassador shouted and denied it, he insisted on his impression. When Horst ended his action, the Ambassador said to him angrily that he must have him posted or send him to the South Pole. Horst explained that Escalopia had broken off diplomatic relations with South Pole so that he was not able to work there. The Ambassador was more angry hearing this. He sorted his messy clothes and went out without saying a word. Horst was pleased with himself that he would not be sent to South Pole.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| While hearing | "While hearing" is unnatural – "On hearing" or "When he heard" is better | On hearing |
| he aimed him a fire extinguisher | Wrong preposition/verb structure – "aimed a fire extinguisher at him" is correct | aimed a fire extinguisher at him |
| he was on fire | Ambiguous – "he" could refer to the Ambassador or Horst; context suggests Horst is referring to the Ambassador, so "the Ambassador was on fire" is clearer | the Ambassador was on fire |
| he insisted on his impression | "Impression" is vague – "insisted on what he saw" or "insisted he was right" is clearer | he insisted on what he saw |
| ended his action | Vague and awkward – "finished" or "stopped spraying" is better | finished spraying him |
| must have him posted or send him | Verb form error after "have him" – "posted" is past participle; but "send" should be "send" (base form after "have him") – actually the structure should be consistent: "have him posted or sent" | have him posted or sent |
| broken off diplomatic relations with South Pole | Missing "the" – "with the South Pole" (as a region) or "with the country" – but "South Pole" is a place; better: "with the country" or "with that region" | broken off diplomatic relations with the South Pole |
| so that he was not able | "So that" implies purpose – "so he was not able" (result) is better | so he was not able |
| The Ambassador was more angry hearing this | Word order – "more angry" is fine, but "angrier" is more natural; also "on hearing this" | The Ambassador was angrier on hearing this |
| He sorted his messy clothes | "Sorted" is odd – "straightened" is better | He straightened his messy clothes |
| Horst was pleased with himself that | Awkward structure – "pleased with himself that" – better: "Horst was pleased that" or "pleased with himself because" | Horst was pleased with himself because |
| he would not be sent to South Pole | Missing "the" – "to the South Pole" | he would not be sent to the South Pole |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

**On hearing** that the basement was on fire, the Ambassador went down to see what had happened. However, when his assistant Horst saw him, he **aimed a fire extinguisher at him** and explained that **the Ambassador** was on fire. Though the Ambassador shouted and denied it, **Horst insisted on what he saw**. When Horst **finished spraying him**, the Ambassador said to him angrily that he must have him **posted or sent** to the South Pole. Horst explained that Escalopia had broken off diplomatic relations with **the South Pole**, **so** he was not able to work there. The Ambassador was **angrier on hearing** this. He **straightened** his messy clothes and went out without saying a word. Horst was **pleased with himself because** he would not be sent to **the South Pole**.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

**When** the Ambassador heard that the basement was on fire, he **hurried** down to see what had happened. However, **his assistant Horst** saw him and **immediately aimed** a fire extinguisher at him, **shouting** that the Ambassador was on fire. **Although** the Ambassador **protested** and **said he wasn't**, Horst **refused to believe him**. **After** Horst **finally stopped**, the Ambassador **furiously told** him that he would have him **transferred** – **either** posted **elsewhere or sent** to the South Pole. Horst **quickly** explained that Escalopia had broken off diplomatic relations **with that region**, so he **couldn't** work there **anyway**. **This only made** the Ambassador angrier. **Without another word**, he **straightened** his messy clothes and **stormed out**. Horst, **however**, was **secretly** pleased **because** he wouldn't be sent to the South Pole **after all**.
