## ordinary version:

Two young boys who never smoke before wanted to have a try. One evening after they had supper, their father went into the living room to watch TV, leaving a packet of cigarettes on the table. They took two cigarettes from the packet and went out of the house secretly. They hid in the garage, hoping to enjoy their smoking there. They lit the cigarette and began to smoke, they couldn't help coughing because the smoke was too strong for them.

The father somehow saw the smoke coming from the garage and rushed down quickly. When he saw two boys smoking there, he smile. Instead of criticizing them, he offered them cigars and asked them to have a taste. The boys accepted them. But cigars were stronger than cigarette. When they smoked, they both felt sick and coughed badly. This was a punishment for them.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original                                                     | Problem                                                      | Correction                                                   |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| never smoke                                                  | Wrong tense – "never smoked" for past experience, or "had never smoked" for past perfect | had never smoked                                             |
| wanted to have a try                                         | Slightly unnatural – "wanted to try it" is more concise      | wanted to try it                                             |
| they lit the cigarette and began to smoke, they couldn't help coughing | Comma splice – two independent clauses joined only by a comma | they lit the cigarettes and began to smoke, but they couldn't help coughing |
| the cigarette                                                | Singular inconsistent with "two cigarettes/two boys" – use plural | the cigarettes                                               |
| he smile                                                     | Wrong tense – past tense "smiled" needed                     | he smiled                                                    |
| cigarette                                                    | Singular inconsistent with "cigars" – use plural "cigarettes" | cigarettes                                                   |
| This was a punishment for them                               | Slightly awkward – passive or rephrased version sounds more natural | This served as a punishment for them                         |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

Two young boys who **had never smoked** before wanted to **try it**. One evening after they had supper, their father went into the living room to watch TV, leaving a packet of cigarettes on the table. They took two cigarettes from the packet and went out of the house secretly. They hid in the garage, hoping to enjoy their smoking there. They lit **the cigarettes** and began to smoke, **but** they couldn't help coughing because the smoke was too strong for them.

The father somehow saw the smoke coming from the garage and rushed down quickly. When he saw two boys smoking there, **he smiled**. Instead of criticizing them, he offered them cigars and asked them to have a taste. The boys accepted them. But cigars were stronger than **cigarettes**. When they smoked, they both felt sick and coughed badly. **This served as** a punishment for them.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

Two young boys who **had never smoked** before **decided to try it**. One evening **after supper**, their father went into the living room to watch TV, leaving a packet of cigarettes on the table. They **secretly** took two cigarettes from the packet and went out of the house. They hid in the garage, hoping to enjoy **smoking there**. They lit **the cigarettes** and began to smoke, **but** they couldn't help coughing because the smoke was too strong for them.

The father somehow saw the smoke coming from the garage and **rushed down quickly**. When he saw **the** two boys smoking there, **he smiled**. Instead of criticizing them, he offered them cigars and asked them to **try one**. The boys accepted. But cigars were stronger than **cigarettes**. When they smoked, they both felt sick and coughed **badly**. **That was punishment enough.**
