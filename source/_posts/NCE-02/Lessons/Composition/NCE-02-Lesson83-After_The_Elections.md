## ordinary version:

The annual election conference was held last weekend. A politician, the current prime minister, was giving a pre-election speech. Two big promises were said to be done in the next five years in his speech. First, more houses and schools would be built for poor in the countryside in order to improve the living condition and to balance education level. Second, a better foreign policy would be issued to import foreign industries and factories. This would enhance the employee rate greatly. After ending speech, the asking questions part was coming. Some members of audience asked rude questions. The politician lost his temper when he heard that it was impossible to complete his promises. He even couldn’t help cursing the audience. He said that the audience did not deserve more houses, etc. Then he walked off angrily. Finally, it was no doubt that he was defeated in the election.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| were said to be done | "Be done" is vague – "would be achieved" or "would be fulfilled" is clearer | were said to be fulfilled |
| in the next five years in his speech | Word order awkward – move "in his speech" to the beginning or after "promises" | in his speech, over the next five years |
| for poor | Missing "the" before "poor" (adjective used as noun) | for the poor |
| the living condition | Should be plural "living conditions" (general standard of life) | living conditions |
| to balance education level | Unnatural phrasing – "to balance" is not typical; "to improve/raise education levels" is better | to improve education levels |
| would be issued | Unnatural for policy – "would be introduced" or "would be adopted" | would be introduced |
| to import foreign industries and factories | You import goods, not whole industries – "to attract" is correct | to attract foreign industries and factories |
| employee rate | Wrong term – "employment rate" is correct | employment rate |
| After ending speech | Missing possessive or article – "After ending his speech" | After ending his speech |
| the asking questions part was coming | Very awkward phrasing – "a Q&A session followed" | a Q&A session followed |
| Some members of audience | Missing "the" – "members of the audience" | members of the audience |
| He even couldn't | Word order – "couldn't even" is standard | He couldn't even |
| it was no doubt that | Wrong structure – use "there was no doubt that" | There was no doubt that |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

The annual election conference was held last weekend. A politician, the current prime minister, was giving a pre-election speech. **In his speech**, two big promises were said to be **fulfilled** over the next five years. First, more houses and schools would be built for **the poor** in the countryside in order to improve **living conditions** and to **improve education levels**. Second, a better foreign policy would be **introduced** to **attract** foreign industries and factories. This would enhance the **employment rate** greatly. **After ending his speech**, **a Q&A session followed**. Some **members of the audience** asked rude questions. The politician lost his temper when he heard that it was impossible to **fulfill** his promises. **He couldn't even** help cursing the audience. He said that the audience did not deserve more houses, etc. Then he walked off angrily. Finally, **there was no doubt that** he was defeated in the election.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

The annual election conference **took place** last weekend. **The current prime minister** was giving a pre-election speech **in which he claimed that two major promises would be fulfilled** over the next five years. First, more houses and schools would be built for **the poor** in the countryside to improve **living conditions and education levels**. Second, a **new** foreign policy would be **introduced** to **attract** foreign industries and factories, which would **significantly boost** the **employment rate**.

**After he finished his speech**, a Q&A session followed. Some **audience members** asked rude questions. The politician lost his temper when **people said** it was impossible to **keep** his promises. He **couldn't stop himself from cursing** the audience, **saying** they did not deserve more houses **or better living standards**. Then he walked off angrily. **Unsurprisingly**, he was defeated in the election.
