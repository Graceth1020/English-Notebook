## ordinary version:

After the plane had taken off at 12:15 pm, a passenger who sat in the middle of the plane threw a light cigarette into an air vent which he thought was an ash tray. After throwing, he closed his eyes and prepared to sleep. The sky was so dark that he planed to have a good sleep. However, the smoke had caused a panic. The engines had been fired by the cigarette so as the plane had to return to the airport. The passengers were frightened until the flight attendant had told what had happened. After the plane had touched down, the air engines were being down. The ambulances had been waiting on the air line. Fortunately, nobody was hurt. As the fire was put out successfully, the plane was able to take off again.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original                                    | Problem                                                      | Correction                                              |
| ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| took off at 12:15 pm                        | "pm" is redundant with "12:15" (12:15 PM is fine, but "at 12:15" suffices) | took off at 12:15 (or kept as is – not a serious error) |
| a light cigarette                           | "Light" as an adjective before "cigarette" is unclear – "lit" (burning) is correct | a lit cigarette                                         |
| an air vent                                 | Correct, but ensure consistency – "air vent" is fine         | (no change needed)                                      |
| After throwing                              | Missing object – "after throwing it" sounds more natural     | After throwing it                                       |
| planed                                      | Wrong spelling – "planned" (double n) is correct             | planned                                                 |
| had caused a panic                          | Past perfect not necessary – simple past "caused" is fine, but not wrong | caused (or kept as had caused)                          |
| The engines had been fired by the cigarette | "Fired" is awkward – use "set on fire" or "ignited". Also passive voice is unnatural here | The cigarette had set the engines on fire               |
| so as the plane had to                      | "So as" is incorrect here – use "so" or "so that"            | so the plane had to                                     |
| the flight attendant had told               | Past perfect unnecessary – simple past "told" is fine        | told                                                    |
| the air engines were being down             | "Were being down" is incorrect – "were shut down" or "were turned off" | were shut down                                          |
| on the air line                             | Wrong term – "on the runway" or "on the tarmac" is correct   | on the runway                                           |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

After the plane had taken off at 12:15 pm, a passenger who **was sitting** in the middle of the plane threw **a lit cigarette** into an air vent which he thought was an ashtray. After throwing **it**, he closed his eyes and prepared to sleep. The sky was so dark that he **planned** to have a good sleep. However, the smoke **caused** a panic. **The cigarette had set the engines on fire, so** the plane had to return to the airport. The passengers were frightened until the flight attendant **told** them what had happened. After the plane had touched down, the **engines were shut down**. The ambulances had been waiting **on the runway**. Fortunately, nobody was hurt. As the fire was put out successfully, the plane was able to take off again.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

After the plane **took off** at 12:15 pm, a passenger **sitting** in the middle of the plane threw **a lit cigarette** into an air vent that he thought was an ashtray. **He then closed** his eyes and prepared to sleep. The sky was so dark that he **planned** to have a good sleep. However, the smoke **caused a panic. The cigarette set the engines on fire, so** the plane had to return to the airport. The passengers **remained** frightened until the flight attendant **explained what had happened**. After the plane **touched down**, the **engines were shut down**. **Ambulances** had been waiting **on the runway**. Fortunately, nobody was hurt. **After the fire was put out successfully**, the plane was able to take off again.
