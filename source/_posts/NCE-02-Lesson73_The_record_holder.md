## ordinary version:

After having nothing to eat and wear for a few days, the boy returned home in sick, cold and hungry. During these days when he was away, his parents worried about him. They were looking for him everywhere but could not find him. One day, while they were looking for their son at the harbour, to their surprise, they met him. His mother even could not believe her eyes. After the boy had restored, he returned school with fear. He was afraid of being punished. 

When he entered his classroom, his classmates gave him a hero`s welcome. He felt so shy for admired by his classmates. The boy was asked many questions by them. For example, how did he dare to do this? did not he afraid of being stolen? What did he think when he was sent home? Then, the headmaster announced his punishment which demanded the boy to give a public talk to the whole school about his experiences abroad.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| in sick, cold and hungry | "In" is wrong before adjectives – use "feeling" or remove "in" | returned home sick, cold and hungry |
| During these days when he was away | Wordy – "While he was away" is more concise | While he was away |
| his parents worried about him | Correct | (no change) |
| to their surprise, they met him | "They met him" implies they encountered each other – but the parents found him; "they found him" is clearer | they found him |
| His mother even could not | Word order – "could not even" is correct | His mother could not even |
| After the boy had restored | "Restored" is transitive (needs an object) – he restored *something*; "recovered" is intransitive | After the boy had recovered |
| he returned school | Missing preposition – "returned to school" | he returned to school |
| a hero`s welcome | Wrong apostrophe – should be straight apostrophe: `hero's` | a hero's welcome |
| He felt so shy for admired by his classmates | Wrong structure – "for admired" is incorrect; use "because he was admired" or "for being admired" | He felt so shy because he was admired by his classmates |
| The boy was asked many questions by them | Passive voice is okay but awkward; active voice is more natural | They asked the boy many questions |
| For example, how did he dare to do this? | Correct but note: questions in reported speech should be in indirect form in formal writing (but here it's direct, which is fine for narrative) | (no change – acceptable) |
| did not he afraid of being stolen? | (1) Word order for negative question – "Wasn't he afraid" (2) "Being stolen" – you steal objects, not people; "being kidnapped" is correct | Wasn't he afraid of being kidnapped? |
| What did he think when he was sent home? | Correct | (no change) |
| which demanded the boy to give | Wrong structure – "demanded + that" or "demanded + noun"; "demanded the boy to give" is incorrect | which demanded that the boy give |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

After having nothing to eat and wear for a few days, the boy returned home **sick, cold and hungry**. **While he was away**, his parents worried about him. They were looking for him everywhere but could not find him. One day, while they were looking for their son at the harbour, to their surprise, **they found him**. **His mother could not even** believe her eyes. After the boy had **recovered**, he returned **to** school with fear. He was afraid of being punished.

When he entered his classroom, his classmates gave him **a hero's** welcome. He felt so shy **because he was admired** by his classmates. **They asked** the boy many questions. For example, how did he dare to do this? **Wasn't he afraid** of being **kidnapped**? What did he think when he was sent home? Then, the headmaster announced his punishment, **which demanded that the boy give** a public talk to the whole school about his experiences abroad.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

After **going** nothing to eat **or** wear for a few days, the boy **came** home sick, cold, and hungry. While he was away, his parents **were worried sick about** him. They **searched** everywhere **but in vain**. One day, while they were looking for him at the harbour, **they unexpectedly found** him. His mother **couldn't** even believe her eyes. After the boy **recovered**, he **went back** to school **in fear** of being punished. 

When he entered his classroom, his classmates gave him a hero's welcome. He felt **embarrassed** because **everyone** admired him. They **bombarded** him with questions, such as: How did he dare to do this? Wasn't he afraid of being kidnapped? What went through his mind when he was sent home? Finally, the headmaster announced his punishment – **the boy had to give** a public talk to the whole school about his experiences abroad.
