## The Full Picture

The verb **hang** has **two completely different past-tense forms**, and they depend entirely on **meaning**, not on grammar rules like regular/irregular verbs.

---

### 1. HUNG – for physical suspension (things)

Use **hung** when something is **supported from above** and dangles down.

**Examples:**
- *I hung a new lamp in the dining room.*
- *The laundry hung on the line all day.*
- *His jacket hung behind the door.*
- *Long curtains hung over the windows.*

**Also used figuratively:**
- *She hung her head in shame.* (lowered her head)
- *The decision hung over us all week.* (lingered/remained)

**And for common phrasal verbs:**
- *hang out* (relax): *We hung out at the café.*
- *hang up* (end a call): *He hung up angrily.*
- *hang on* (wait): *She hung on for a few more minutes.*

---

### 2. HANGED – for death by rope (people)

Use **hanged** **only** when referring to **execution or suicide** by rope. This is a legal/historical term, and it's **never** used for objects.

**Examples:**
- *The prisoner was hanged at sunrise.*
- *He hanged himself in despair.*
- *They hanged the traitor in the town square.*

**Important:** If you say *"He was hung,"* it means his body was dangling (maybe after death, but not as a method of execution). If you say *"He was hanged,"* it clearly means he was executed.

---

### Why do we have two forms?

Historically, Old English had **two different verbs**:
- One was regular (for objects) → became *hung*
- One was weak (for executions) → became *hanged*

Over time, they merged into one verb in the present tense, but kept **both past forms** to avoid confusion. English speakers needed a way to tell the difference between *"The flag was suspended"* and *"The criminal was executed."*

---

### Quick Comparison Table

| Context | Present | Past | Past Participle |
|---------|---------|------|-----------------|
| Objects / things | hang | **hung** | **hung** |
| Execution / suicide | hang | **hanged** | **hanged** |

---

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| *He hung himself.* | *He **hanged** himself.* | Suicide = hanged |
| *The picture was hanged.* | *The picture was **hung**.* | Object = hung |
| *The flag hanged on the pole.* | *The flag **hung** on the pole.* | Object = hung |
| *They hung the criminal.* | *They **hanged** the criminal.* | Execution = hanged |

---

### What about computers? (bonus)

When a computer or program **stops responding** (freezes), we say:

- *My computer **hung** again.* (past tense)
- *The system has **hung**.* (past participle)

We **never** say *hanged* for computers—that would sound like the computer was killed on purpose!

---

### One Last Memory Trick (the most famous one)

> **"Men are hanged; pictures are hung."**

Or even simpler:

> **If it's dead, it's hanged. If it's not, it's hung.**

---

## Final Summary

| Use this | When |
|----------|------|
| **hung** | Coats, pictures, hair, lights, laundry, hanging out, hanging up, computers freezing |
| **hanged** | Execution, suicide, death penalty by rope |
