# Quantity Words: Dozen, Score, and Other Special Counters

This note covers words that behave like *hundred/thousand/million/billion* but have their own unique rules and usage patterns.

---

## The Golden Rule (Same as Before)

> **If you put a specific number before it → NO "s"**  
> **If you mean "many" without a specific number → ADD "s" + "of"**

---

## 1. Dozen (12)

| Usage | Countable? | Example | Rule |
|-------|------------|---------|------|
| With a specific number (1, 2, 5 dozen) | ❌ **Uncountable** (no -s) | *two **dozen** eggs* | Dozen stays singular |
| Without a number, meaning "many groups of 12" | ✅ **Countable** (with -s) | ***dozens of** people* | Add -s and use "of" |
| As a noun meaning "a set of 12" | ✅ **Countable** | *He bought three **dozens*** (rare, but possible) | Can be plural |

**Examples:**
- *I need **three dozen** cookies for the party.* (specific → no -s)
- ***Dozens of** volunteers showed up.* (vague → add -s)
- *She ordered **a dozen** roses.* (a = one → no -s)

**Special Note:** *Dozen* is very common in everyday speech, especially for food, flowers, and counting items.

---

## 2. Score (20)

| Usage | Countable? | Example | Rule |
|-------|------------|---------|------|
| With a specific number (1, 2, 5 score) | ❌ **Uncountable** (no -s) | *four **score** and seven years* | Score stays singular |
| Without a number, meaning "many groups of 20" | ✅ **Countable** (with -s) | ***scores of** people* | Add -s and use "of" |
| As a noun meaning "a set of 20" | ✅ **Countable** | *They came in **scores*** | Can be plural |

**Examples:**
- *Four **score** and seven years ago...* (famous quote from Lincoln → specific)
- ***Scores of** fans waited outside.* (vague → add -s)
- *The crowd arrived in **scores**.* (vague plural noun)

**Special Note:** *Score* is **very old-fashioned** in modern English. You mostly see it in:
- Historical or biblical texts.
- The famous Gettysburg Address: *"Four score and seven years ago..."* (= 87 years)
- Literary writing.

In daily conversation, people say **twenty** instead.

---

## 3. Gross (144) — (12 dozen)

| Usage | Countable? | Example | Rule |
|-------|------------|---------|------|
| With a specific number (1, 2, 5 gross) | ❌ **Uncountable** (no -s) | *three **gross** of pencils* | Gross stays singular |
| Without a number, meaning "many groups of 144" | ✅ **Countable** (with -s) | ***grosses of** buttons* | Add -s and use "of" |

**Examples:**
- *The factory ordered **two gross** of screws.* (specific → no -s)
- ***Grosses of** buttons were produced.* (vague → add -s)

**Special Note:** *Gross* is **very specialized** — used in:
- Manufacturing (counting small items like screws, buttons, nails).
- Wholesale business.
- You will almost never hear it in everyday conversation.

---

## 4. Couple (2)

| Usage | Countable? | Example | Rule |
|-------|------------|---------|------|
| With a specific number (only "a" or "one") | ❌ **Uncountable** (no -s) | *a **couple** of apples* | Couple stays singular |
| Without a number, meaning "a few" | ❌ **Uncountable** (no -s) | *I'll be there in **a couple** of minutes* | Still singular |
| As a noun meaning "two people in a relationship" | ✅ **Countable** | *The **couples** arrived together.* | Can be plural |

**Examples:**
- *I have **a couple** of questions.* (≈ two or a few)
- *There were three **couples** at the restaurant.* (three pairs of people)

**Special Note:**
- *A couple* **always** takes "of" when followed by a noun: *a couple of apples*.
- In informal speech, *a couple* can mean "a few" (not exactly 2).

---

## 5. Few / Several / Many — (Not Exact Numbers)

These are **not exact quantity words** — they are **determiners** (like adjectives). They **never** take -s.

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **few** | a small number | *a few people* |
| **several** | more than two, not many | *several books* |
| **many** | a large number | *many students* |

**Note:** These are **never** used with "of" unless you specify a group:
- *few of them* ✅ (specific group)
- *several of the students* ✅ (specific group)
- *many of us* ✅ (specific group)

---

## 6. Pair (2 items that go together)

| Usage | Countable? | Example | Rule |
|-------|------------|---------|------|
| Meaning "two matching items" | ✅ **Countable** | *a **pair** of shoes* | Singular (one pair) |
| Plural meaning "multiple pairs" | ✅ **Countable** | *two **pairs** of socks* | Add -s for plural |

**Examples:**
- *I bought **a pair** of jeans.* (singular)
- *She has three **pairs** of glasses.* (plural)

**Special Note:** *Pair* is a **regular countable noun** — it follows normal plural rules.

## Complete Comparison Table

| Quantity Word | Meaning | With Specific Number | Vague / General (With -s) | As a Plural Noun |
|---------------|---------|----------------------|---------------------------|------------------|
| **dozen** | 12 | *two **dozen*** | ***dozens of*** | *bought in **dozens*** |
| **score** | 20 | *four **score*** | ***scores of*** | *came in **scores*** |
| **gross** | 144 | *three **gross*** | ***grosses of*** | *packed in **grosses*** |
| **couple** | 2 | *a **couple*** | ❌ (never plural) | *three **couples*** (people) |
| **pair** | 2 matching items | *a **pair*** | ❌ (regular noun) | *five **pairs*** |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| *three **dozens** eggs* | *three **dozen** eggs* | Specific number → no -s |
| ***dozen of** eggs* | ***dozens of** eggs* | No specific number → add -s + "of" |
| *four **scores** years* | *four **score** years* | Specific number → no -s |
| ***score of** people* | ***scores of** people* | No specific number → add -s + "of" |
| *two **couple** of apples* | *two **couples** of apples* (if meaning two groups) OR *a couple of apples* | *Couple* is singular with "a" |
| *a **couple** apples* | *a **couple of** apples* | *Couple* always needs "of" before a noun |

---

## Special Case: "A Dozen" vs. "Dozens of"

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| ***a dozen*** | exactly 12 (or roughly 12) | *I bought a dozen eggs.* |
| ***dozens of*** | many groups of 12 (vague, large number) | *Dozens of birds flew overhead.* |

**Same pattern applies to:**
- *a score* = exactly 20 → *a score of men*
- *scores of* = many groups of 20 → *scores of men*

---

## Quick Reference: When to Use "of"

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **With a specific number** → NO "of" (except "couple") | *three **dozen** eggs* (NOT *three dozen of eggs*) |
| **With plural form** → USE "of" | ***dozens of** eggs* |
| **With "a"** → "of" is optional OR required | *a dozen eggs* OR *a dozen of them* (specific group) |
| **With "couple"** → ALWAYS "of" | *a couple of eggs* (NEVER *a couple eggs*) |

---

## Special Quirks Summary

| Word | Quirk |
|------|-------|
| **dozen** | Very common. Used for food, flowers, and counting items. |
| **score** | Old-fashioned/Literary. Avoid in daily conversation. |
| **gross** | Very specialized. Used in manufacturing/wholesale only. |
| **couple** | Always uses "of." Can mean "a few" informally. |
| **pair** | Regular countable noun. Always takes -s in plural. |

---

## The One Rule to Remember Forever

> **If there's a number in front → NO -s, NO "of"** → *three dozen eggs*  
> **If there's NO number → ADD -s, ADD "of"** → *dozens of eggs*

**Exception:** *Couple* always uses "of" — *a couple of eggs*.

## Practice Sentences (Test Yourself)

Fill in the blank with the correct form:

1. *I need two ______ (dozen) cupcakes for the bake sale.*
2. *______ (dozen) of protesters gathered in the square.*
3. *Four ______ (score) and seven years ago...*
4. *______ (score) of fans lined up for tickets.*
5. *The factory ordered three ______ (gross) of nails.*
6. *I have a ______ (couple) of questions for you.*
7. *She bought five ______ (pair) of shoes.*

---

**Answers:**
1. **dozen** (specific number → no -s)
2. **Dozens** (no number → add -s + "of")
3. **score** (specific number → no -s)
4. **Scores** (no number → add -s + "of")
5. **gross** (specific number → no -s)
6. **couple** (always "a couple of")
7. **pairs** (regular countable plural)
