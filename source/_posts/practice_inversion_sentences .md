## Practice Exercises Part

### Exercise 1: Rewrite the Sentences (Negative Adverbials)
*Rewrite the sentences starting with the word in brackets.*

1. I have never been so embarrassed. (Never)
2. He rarely eats vegetables. (Rarely)
3. We had no sooner arrived than the show began. (No sooner)
4. He is not only intelligent, but he is also kind. (Not only)
5. She didn't apologize at any point. (At no point)

---

### Exercise 2: Rewrite with "Only"
*Rewrite these sentences beginning with "Only".*

1. You can succeed if you try hard. (Only by)
2. I realized my mistake after I had finished. (Only after)
3. She understood the meaning now. (Only now)
4. We can solve this problem with a miracle. (Only with)

---

### Exercise 3: Complete the Inversion (Conditionals & "So/Such")
*Fill in the gaps with the correct verb form.*

1. ____________ (Had / Have) I known you were sick, I would have visited.
2. ____________ (Should / Would) you need any help, call me immediately.
3. So beautiful ____________ (was / were) the painting that everyone stopped to stare.
4. Such ____________ (was / were) the weather that all flights were canceled.

---

### Exercise 4: Correct the Mistakes
*Find and correct the inversion errors in these sentences.*

1. Never I have seen such a mess.
2. Only by practice you will become fluent.
3. No sooner had she left than the package arrived. (This is correct. Identify if any are wrong).
4. Under no circumstances we should give up.
5. Rarely she goes out after dark.

---

### Exercise 5: Mixed Transformations
*Rewrite the following sentences using inversion.*

1. If I were you, I wouldn't do that.
2. A beautiful garden lay in front of the house. (In front)
3. He didn't realize the danger until it was too late. (Not until)
4. The chef is so skilled that he can cook anything. (So skilled)

---

## Part 3: Answer Key

**Exercise 1**
1. Never have I been so embarrassed.
2. Rarely does he eat vegetables.
3. No sooner had we arrived than the show began.
4. Not only is he intelligent, but he is also kind.
5. At no point did she apologize.

**Exercise 2**
1. Only by trying hard can you succeed.
2. Only after I had finished did I realize my mistake.
3. Only now does she understand the meaning.
4. Only with a miracle can we solve this problem.

**Exercise 3**
1. **Had** I known...
2. **Should** you need...
3. So beautiful **was** the painting...
4. Such **was** the weather...

**Exercise 4**
1. **Never have I** seen such a mess. (Auxiliary before subject)
2. Only by practice **will you** become fluent. (Subject after auxiliary)
3. Correct! (No correction needed).
4. Under no circumstances **should we** give up. (Aux before subject)
5. Rarely **does she** go out after dark. (Add 'does').

**Exercise 5**
1. Were I you, I wouldn't do that.
2. In front of the house lay a beautiful garden.
3. Not until it was too late did he realize the danger.
4. So skilled is the chef that he can cook anything.
