##  Practice Exercises Part

### Exercise 1: Choose the Correct Verb (Proximity vs. Distance)
*Circle the correct verb in brackets.*

1. Neither the president nor the board members (agrees / agree) with the decision.
2. The collection of rare stamps (is / are) worth millions.
3. Not only the children but also their father (love / loves) hiking.
4. The actor, along with his agent, (was / were) at the premiere.
5. Either the cats or the dog (has / have) eaten my sandwich.
6. There (is / are) a sofa and two chairs in the living room.
7. The committee (has / have) decided to postpone the meeting until next week. (Acting as a unit)
8. The jury (is / are) still debating among themselves. (Acting as individuals)

## Part 3: Answer Key

**Exercise 1**
1. **agree** (Proximity – *members* is plural and closest).
2. **is** (Distance – head noun *collection* is singular).
3. **loves** (Proximity – *father* is singular and closest).
4. **was** (Distance – head noun *actor* is singular; ignore *along with*).
5. **has** (Proximity – *dog* is singular and closest).
6. **are** (Proximity – or informal usage; *chairs* is plural and closest. *Note: Formal purists might use "is" for grammatical structure, but "are" is common*).
7. **has** (Distance – acting as a single unit).
8. **are** (Proximity to the idea of individuals – "among themselves" indicates separate actions).

**Exercise 2**
1. The list of items on the table **is** too long. (Distance – head noun is *list*).
2. Neither the manager nor his assistants **are** available. (Proximity – *assistants* is plural).
3. She is one of the few writers who **have** won multiple awards. (Distance in relative clause – *who* refers to *writers*, plural).
4. The president, together with his advisors, **has** left. (Distance – head noun *president*).
5. Every one of the students **has** submitted. (Distance – *Every one* is singular).

**Exercise 3**
1. **has** (Each = singular).
2. **are** (Proximity – *students* is plural).
3. **is** (Distance – head noun *danger*).
4. **were** (Proximity – *reasons* is plural and closest).
5. **has** (Many a = always singular).
6. **are** (Indicates individual actions – "their uniforms").
7. **are** (Proximity – *sisters* is plural).
8. **are** (Distance – head noun *effects* is plural).

**Exercise 4**
1. **Neither the child nor the children are playing outside.** (Proximity – *children* is plural).
2. **The teacher, along with her students, is strict.** (Distance – head noun *teacher* is singular).
3. **There is a pizza and a salad.** (Proximity – *salad* is singular and closest; or use "There are a pizza and a salad" if treating as a plural list).
