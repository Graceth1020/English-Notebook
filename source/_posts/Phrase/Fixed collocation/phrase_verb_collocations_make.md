## MAKE

| Phrasal Verb | Definition | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **make up** | 1. To invent a story, excuse, or lie. <br> 2. To reconcile after an argument. <br> 3. To apply cosmetics. <br> 4. To constitute or form a whole. | 1. She **made up** an excuse for being late. <br> 2. They fought, but they **made up** the next day. <br> 3. She spends 20 minutes **making up** her face every morning. <br> 4. Women **make up** 60% of the workforce. |
| **make out** | 1. To see, hear, or understand with difficulty. <br> 2. To write or complete a document (especially a check). <br> 3. (Informal) To kiss and touch passionately. <br> 4. To claim or pretend (often falsely). | 1. I can't **make out** the sign from here—it's too far. <br> 2. Please **make out** the check to "ABC Company." <br> 3. The teenagers were **making out** on the bench. <br> 4. He **makes out** that he's rich, but he's actually broke. |
| **make of** | 1. To form an opinion or understanding about something. <br> 2. To create or produce from a material. | 1. What do you **make of** his strange behavior? <br> 2. The table is **made of** solid oak. |
| **make for** | 1. To move toward a place. <br> 2. To contribute to or result in (a positive outcome). | 1. The ship **made for** the nearest port during the storm. <br> 2. Hard work and honesty **make for** a successful career. |
| **make up for** | To compensate for a loss, mistake, or deficiency. | I bought her flowers to **make up for** forgetting our anniversary. |
| **make over** | 1. To transfer ownership legally. <br> 2. To give a new appearance, style, or renovation. | 1. He **made over** his estate to his eldest son. <br> 2. She **made over** her old dress into a stylish skirt. |
| **make off** | To leave hurriedly, especially after stealing or doing something wrong. | The thieves **made off** with the stolen jewelry. |
| **make out to** | To write a check, bill, or document in someone's name. | Please **make the check out to** "ABC Company." |

## Final Note on Phrasal Verbs

- **Transitive vs. Intransitive:** Some phrasal verbs take a direct object (*put off the meeting*), while others do not (*get up*).
- **Separable vs. Inseparable:** With many transitive phrasal verbs, you can put the object between the verb and the particle (*take your shoes off* = *take off your shoes*).
- **Context is key:** Many phrasal verbs have multiple meanings depending on the situation—always pay attention to the context!
