## DRAW

| Phrasal Verb | Definition | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **draw up** | 1. To prepare a formal document, plan, or list. <br> 2. (For a vehicle) to stop or pull up to a place. <br> 3. To straighten one's posture. | 1. The lawyers **drew up** the contract. <br> 2. A car **drew up** outside the house. <br> 3. She **drew herself up** and walked away proudly. |
| **draw back** | 1. To retreat or move backward. <br> 2. To pull away in fear, shock, or disgust. <br> 3. To withdraw from a commitment. | 1. The child **drew back** in fear when the dog barked. <br> 2. He **drew back** his hand from the hot stove. <br> 3. The company **drew back** from the deal. |
| **draw in** | 1. To become shorter or darker (days/nights). <br> 2. To involve someone in a situation. <br> 3. To attract or entice. <br> 4. To inhale or suck in. | 1. The days are **drawing in** as winter approaches. <br> 2. I don't want to be **drawn in** to their argument. <br> 3. The circus **drew in** huge crowds. <br> 4. She **drew in** a deep breath. |
| **draw out** | 1. To make someone more talkative or confident. <br> 2. To prolong or lengthen something. <br> 3. To extract or pull out. | 1. The teacher skillfully **drew out** the shy student. <br> 2. He **drew out** the story for too long. <br> 3. The dentist **drew out** the infected tooth. |
| **draw on** | 1. To use as a resource or inspiration. <br> 2. To approach in time (usually with *draw on* = approach). <br> 3. To put on (clothing) slowly. | 1. The author **drew on** his own life experiences for the novel. <br> 2. Winter is **drawing on** – it's getting colder. <br> 3. She **drew on** her gloves before going out. |
| **draw off** | 1. To divert or extract something (attention, liquid, etc.). <br> 2. To remove or drain. | 1. The magician **drew off** the audience's attention with his assistant. <br> 2. They **drew off** some water from the reservoir. |
| **draw together** | 1. To unite or bring people closer. <br> 2. To come closer as a group. | 1. The tragedy **drew the community together**. <br> 2. The team **drew together** to meet the deadline. |
| **draw away** | 1. To move away or pull away from someone/something. <br> 2. To attract or entice someone away. | 1. She **drew away** from him when he tried to hug her. <br> 2. The new job **drew him away** from his hometown. |

## Final Note on Phrasal Verbs

- **Transitive vs. Intransitive:** Some phrasal verbs take a direct object (*put off the meeting*), while others do not (*get up*).
- **Separable vs. Inseparable:** With many transitive phrasal verbs, you can put the object between the verb and the particle (*take your shoes off* = *take off your shoes*).
- **Context is key:** Many phrasal verbs have multiple meanings depending on the situation—always pay attention to the context!
