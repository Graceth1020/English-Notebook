## CALL

| Phrasal Verb | Definition | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **call off** | 1. To cancel an event or arrangement. <br> 2. To stop or abandon an action. | 1. The game was **called off** because of heavy rain. <br> 2. The search was **called off** after dark. |
| **call on** | 1. To visit someone (briefly). <br> 2. To ask someone to speak or answer (especially in class). <br> 3. To formally request or appeal to someone. | 1. I'll **call on** you later this evening. <br> 2. The teacher **called on** me to answer the question. <br> 3. The charity **called on** the public to donate. |
| **call for** | 1. To require or demand (a quality or action). <br> 2. To come and get someone or something. <br> 3. To shout or ask for something. | 1. The recipe **calls for** butter and eggs. <br> 2. I'll **call for** you at 8 PM. <br> 3. He **called for** help, but no one came. |
| **call back** | 1. To return a phone call. <br> 2. To summon someone to return. | 1. I missed his call—I'll **call him back** right away. <br> 2. She **called back** the employees for a meeting. |
| **call in** | 1. To summon someone, especially an expert or professional. <br> 2. To report by phone (e.g., sick leave). <br> 3. To ask for the return of a loan. | 1. They had to **call in** a specialist. <br> 2. I'll **call in** sick tomorrow. <br> 3. The bank **called in** the loan. |
| **call up** | 1. To telephone someone. <br> 2. To summon for military service. <br> 3. To recall or bring to mind. | 1. I'll **call you up** later. <br> 2. The government **called up** the reserves. <br> 3. The photo **called up** memories of my childhood. |
| **call out** | 1. To shout or say something loudly. <br> 2. To challenge or expose someone publicly (often for wrongdoing). <br> 3. To summon someone to help. | 1. She **called out** my name from across the street. <br> 2. He was **called out** for lying. <br> 3. An ambulance was **called out** to the scene. |
| **call upon** | 1. To formally ask or appeal to someone. <br> 2. To visit someone (formal, similar to *call on*). | 1. The committee **called upon** him to resign. <br> 2. We **called upon** my grandmother on Sunday. |

## Final Note on Phrasal Verbs

- **Transitive vs. Intransitive:** Some phrasal verbs take a direct object (*put off the meeting*), while others do not (*get up*).
- **Separable vs. Inseparable:** With many transitive phrasal verbs, you can put the object between the verb and the particle (*take your shoes off* = *take off your shoes*).
- **Context is key:** Many phrasal verbs have multiple meanings depending on the situation—always pay attention to the context!
