## KEEP

| Phrasal Verb | Definition | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **keep up** | 1. To maintain the same pace, level, or standard (often with *with*). <br> 2. To continue doing something. <br> 3. To prevent someone from going to bed. | 1. You're walking too fast—I can't **keep up**. <br> 2. **Keep up** the good work! <br> 3. The noise **kept up** the neighbors all night. |
| **keep on** | To continue doing something (often despite difficulty or repetition). | He **kept on** trying until he succeeded. |
| **keep off** | 1. To stay away from; to avoid stepping on or touching. <br> 2. To prevent something from touching or affecting. | 1. Please **keep off** the grass. <br> 2. A hat will **keep off** the sun. |
| **keep from** | 1. To prevent someone from doing something. <br> 2. To refrain from doing something. | 1. The bad weather **kept us from** going out. <br> 2. I couldn't **keep from** laughing at his joke. |
| **keep out** | 1. To not enter. <br> 2. To prevent someone or something from entering. | 1. The sign said "**Keep out**" – it was private property. <br> 2. The fence **keeps out** wild animals. |
| **keep to** | 1. To adhere to a plan, schedule, or path. <br> 2. To stay on topic. <br> 3. To avoid straying from a subject. | 1. Please **keep to** your budget. <br> 2. **Keep to** the right side of the road. <br> 3. Let's **keep to** the point of this meeting. |
| **keep back** | 1. To hold, retain, or not give something. <br> 2. To hide or withhold information. <br> 3. To stay at a distance. | 1. She **kept back** some money for emergencies. <br> 2. He **kept back** the truth from his parents. <br> 3. The crowd was told to **keep back** from the crime scene. |
| **keep down** | 1. To control, suppress, or prevent from rising. <br> 2. To not vomit (food/drink). <br> 3. To keep noise levels low. | 1. The government tried to **keep down** prices. <br> 2. He couldn't **keep down** his food after the surgery. <br> 3. Please **keep the volume down** – the baby is sleeping. |
| **keep in** | 1. To confine someone indoors (as punishment). <br> 2. To hold back emotions or feelings. <br> 3. To stay informed (with *keep in touch*). | 1. The teacher **kept the students in** after class. <br> 2. He couldn't **keep in** his anger. <br> 3. Let's **keep in** touch after you move. |
| **keep up with** | 1. To stay at the same level or pace as someone/something. <br> 2. To stay informed about news or trends. <br> 3. To maintain communication or a relationship. | 1. It's hard to **keep up with** his energy. <br> 2. I try to **keep up with** the latest technology. <br> 3. I still **keep up with** my college friends. |

## Final Note on Phrasal Verbs

- **Transitive vs. Intransitive:** Some phrasal verbs take a direct object (*put off the meeting*), while others do not (*get up*).
- **Separable vs. Inseparable:** With many transitive phrasal verbs, you can put the object between the verb and the particle (*take your shoes off* = *take off your shoes*).
- **Context is key:** Many phrasal verbs have multiple meanings depending on the situation—always pay attention to the context!
