### Level 1: Fill in the Blank (Basic Choice)

*Choose the best conjunction from the box below. **Hint:** Think about whether the reason is the main point, background info, or an afterthought.*
**Options:** *because / as / for / now that*

1. I didn't eat dinner __________ I wasn't hungry at all. (The hunger is the main, essential reason.)
2. __________ we've finished our homework, we can go play outside. (A new situation has just occurred.)
3. She decided to walk to work, __________ she needed the exercise. (This is a formal, literary afterthought added after the main clause.)
4. __________ it was getting dark, we turned on the headlights. (The darkness is obvious background info; the action is the main point.)

---

**Answers for Level 1:**

1. **because** (Strongest emphasis on the direct reason.)
2. **Now that** (A new circumstance—finishing homework—triggers the new action.)
3. **for** (Formal afterthought, placed after a comma in the middle of the sentence.)
4. **As** (Sets the scene with obvious background info at the start.)

### Level 2: Sentence Rewriting (Choosing the Right Nuance)

*Rewrite the following ideas using the conjunction given in parentheses. You may need to change the word order or add/remove small words to make it sound natural.*

1. **(Since)** - *You are the team leader. You should make the final decision.*
  - *Rewrite:* ______________________________________________

2. **(Seeing that)** - *The restaurant is closed. We should cook at home instead.*
  - *Rewrite:* ______________________________________________

3. **(Inasmuch as)** - *The data is accurate. We trust the research findings.*
  - *Rewrite:* ______________________________________________

4. **(For)** - *I lent him my car. He is my brother.*
  - *Rewrite:* ______________________________________________

---

**Answers for Level 2:**

1. **Since you are the team leader, you should make the final decision.** (Treats your leadership as a known, logical fact.)
2. **Seeing that the restaurant is closed, we should cook at home instead.** (States an obvious fact leading to a logical conclusion—very conversational.)
3. **Inasmuch as the data is accurate, we trust the research findings.** (Very formal/academic—the accuracy is the specific reason for the trust.)
4. **I lent him my car, for he is my brother.** (Remember: "for" always comes in the middle after a comma, never at the start!)

### Level 3: Spot the Error & Explain Why

*Each of these sentences contains a mistake in the use of a cause conjunction. Identify the error and correct it.*

1. **Error:** *For it was raining, we postponed the match.*
  - **What's wrong?** _____________________________________
  - **Correction:** _____________________________________

2. **Error:** *I stayed home due to I felt sick.*
  - **What's wrong?** _____________________________________
  - **Correction:** _____________________________________

3. **Error:** *As I really needed money, so I took the night shift.*
  - **What's wrong?** _____________________________________
  - **Correction:** _____________________________________

4. **Error:** *Now that the store was closed yesterday, I couldn't buy milk.*
  - **What's wrong?** _____________________________________
  - **Correction:** _____________________________________

---

**Answers for Level 3:**

1. **What's wrong?** *"For"* cannot start a sentence; it is only used in the middle as an afterthought.  
   **Correction:** ***Because** it was raining, we postponed the match.* (or *As it was raining...*)

2. **What's wrong?** *"Due to"* is a preposition and must be followed by a **noun**, not a full clause (subject + verb).  
   **Correction:** *I stayed home **because** I felt sick.* (or *...due to **feeling** sick* – using a gerund/noun).

3. **What's wrong?** You cannot use **both** "as/since/because" and "so" in the same sentence—they are redundant.  
   **Correction:** *As I really needed money, I took the night shift.* (Remove the "so".)

4. **What's wrong?** *"Now that"* is used for a **new, current** situation that has just changed. "Yesterday" is in the past and implies a one-time event, not a new ongoing circumstance.  
   **Correction:** ***Because** the store was closed yesterday, I couldn't buy milk.* (or *Since*).

### 💡 Bonus Challenge (Mixed Conjunctions)

*Fill in the blanks with the **most appropriate** conjunction (there may be more than one correct answer, but pick the BEST one for the context).*

1. __________ you're not going to eat that pizza, can I have it? (Obvious, conversational deduction)
2. The experiment was considered a failure, __________ the initial hypothesis was flawed. (Formal, academic, specific reason)
3. __________ the sun had set, the temperature dropped quickly. (Simple background info)
4. I apologized to her, __________ I had clearly offended her. (Literary afterthought)

---

**Answers for Bonus:**

1. **Seeing that** (Best for an obvious, slightly informal deduction)
2. **inasmuch as** (Best for formal/academic writing)
3. **As** (Best for setting the scene with background info)
4. **for** (Best for a literary, explanatory afterthought)

---

**How did you do?** If you got all of Level 3 correct, you've mastered the nuances! If you struggled with any, focus on remembering these three golden rules:

- **"Because" = Essential reason** (put it in the middle).
- **"As/Since" = Known background** (put it at the start).
- **"For" = Formal afterthought** (put it in the middle, after a comma, never at the start).
