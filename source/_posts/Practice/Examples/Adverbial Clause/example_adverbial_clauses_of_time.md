# Dialogue

## Dialogue 1: Morning Routine (Mom & Son)

**Mom:** Wake up, sweetie! It's 7 AM.

**Son:** Can I sleep five more minutes?

**Mom:** No, **as soon as you wake up**, you need to get ready. You have school.

**Son:** Okay, okay. I'm getting up.

**Mom:** Good. **While you're brushing your teeth**, I'll pack your lunch.

*[10 minutes later]*

**Son:** Mom, where's my blue shirt?

**Mom:** I washed it. **When it's dry**, I'll put it in your closet. For now, wear the white one.

**Son:** Fine. Can I have breakfast now?

**Mom:** **After you make your bed**, yes. Don't forget—**before you leave**, you need to feed the cat.

**Son:** I know. I'll feed her **after I eat**.

**Mom:** Okay. Hurry up, **until you're ready** I'll wait in the car. Don't be late!

**Son:** Got it!

## Dialogue 2: At a Restaurant (Waiter & Customer)

**Waiter:** Good evening. Are you ready to order?

**Customer:** Almost. We're still looking.

**Waiter:** No problem. **Whenever you're ready**, just let me know.

**Customer:** Actually, can you recommend something?

**Waiter:** Sure. **While you're deciding**, I can tell you about our specials.

**Customer:** That would be great.

**Waiter:** We have a grilled salmon today. **Once you try it**, you'll love it—it's fresh.

**Customer:** Sounds good. I'll take that.

**Waiter:** And for you, ma'am?

**Wife:** I'm not sure yet. Can I order **after my husband gets his food**? I want to see how big the portions are.

**Waiter:** Of course. **As soon as his dish arrives**, I'll come back for your order.

**Wife:** Perfect. Thank you.

## Dialogue 3: Airport & Travel (Ticket Agent & Passenger)

**Agent:** Good morning. May I see your passport?

**Passenger:** Here you go.

**Agent:** Thank you. **Before you board the plane**, you'll need to go through security.

**Passenger:** I know. I've already checked in online.

**Agent:** Great. **Once you pass security**, your gate will be on the screen. Gate B23.

**Passenger:** And what time does boarding start?

**Agent:** Boarding starts **as soon as the plane is ready**. Usually about 30 minutes before departure.

**Passenger:** Okay. Can I buy a coffee **while I wait**?

**Agent:** Sure. There's a café **after you go through security**.

**Passenger:** Perfect. Also, I have a connecting flight.

**Agent:** Yes. **When you arrive in Tokyo**, you'll have 2 hours before your next flight.

**Passenger:** That's enough time.

**Agent:** One more thing—**until you reach your final destination**, keep your baggage claim tags with you.

**Passenger:** Got it. Thank you!

## Dialogue 4: Work & Meetings (Boss & Employee)

**Boss:** We need to finish this project by Friday.

**Employee:** I understand. I'll work on it today.

**Boss:** Good. **Whenever you have a question**, just ask me.

**Employee:** Actually, I have a question now. Can I send the draft **before you leave** tonight?

**Boss:** Yes, **as long as it's complete**. I mean, **once you finish the main sections**, send it to me. I'll review it **while I'm at home**.

**Employee:** Great. I'll work on it now. **Until I hear back from you**, I won't move to the next phase.

**Boss:** That's smart. **By the time you finish the draft**, I'll probably be in a meeting. But I'll read it **as soon as I'm free**.

**Employee:** Okay. And **after I get your feedback**, I'll make the revisions.

**Boss:** Perfect. Let's get to work!

# Examples

Absolutely! Here is a **complete list** of every common time conjunction, each with **one clear example sentence** so you can see them all side by side.

## All Time Conjunctions – One Example Each

| Conjunction | Example Sentence |
|-------------|------------------|
| **when** | *I'll call you **when** I arrive at the airport.* |
| **while** | *She listened to music **while** she was cooking dinner.* |
| **as** | *He waved goodbye **as** the train pulled away.* |
| **after** | *We went for a walk **after** we finished our meal.* |
| **before** | *Please turn off the lights **before** you leave the room.* |
| **until / till** | *Stay in bed **until** the doctor comes to see you.* |
| **since** | *I've known her **since** we were in elementary school.* |
| **as soon as** | *Send me a message **as soon as** you land in London.* |
| **once** | *You'll feel much better **once** you take this medicine.* |
| **whenever** | *She brings me flowers **whenever** she visits.* |
| **by the time** | *By the time** we got to the cinema, the movie had already started.* |
| **every time** | *Every time** I drink coffee late at night, I can't sleep.* |
| **now that** | *Now that** the kids are asleep, we can watch our show.* (time + reason) |
| **the moment (that)** | *The moment** she walked in, everyone stopped talking.* |
| **as long as** | *I'll stay here **as long as** you need me.* (time, not condition) |
| **no sooner...than** | *No sooner** had I sat down **than** the phone rang.* (formal) |
| **hardly...when** | *Hardly** had we left home **when** it started to rain.* (formal) |

---

## Quick Note on Formal Ones

- **no sooner...than** and **hardly...when** are **inverted** structures – they use *had + subject* (not *subject + had*). They are common in writing but rare in daily speech.
- **the moment (that)** and **as long as** are very common in everyday English.

## All 17 in a Short Story (to see them in context)

> *I met my wife in college. **Since** we were freshmen, we had been friends. **Whenever** I saw her in the library, I smiled. **Every time** she laughed, I felt happy. One day, **as** I was walking to class, she waved at me. **The moment** she said my name, I knew I liked her. **By the time** I realized it, I was already in love. **Before** I could stop myself, I asked her out. We went on a date **after** our final exam. **While** we were eating dinner, we talked for hours. She told me she had known **as soon as** we met that we would be together. **Once** she said that, I felt so relieved. **Now that** we're married, I look back and smile. **As long as** I live, I'll never forget that day. **Hardly** had we finished dinner **when** she said yes to a second date. **No sooner** had I dropped her home **than** I called my mom to tell her the news. I waited **until** midnight to fall asleep. I'll love her **till** the end of my life.*

## Summary Checklist

| Conjunction | Used For | One-Liner Example |
|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| **when** | specific moment | *Call me when you're free.* |
| **while** | during an ongoing action | *He read while eating.* |
| **as** | two simultaneous actions | *She smiled as she spoke.* |
| **after** | one event follows another | *We left after the show.* |
| **before** | one event precedes another | *Think before you speak.* |
| **until/till** | up to a point | *Wait until I come back.* |
| **since** | from a past point | *I've been here since 8 AM.* |
| **as soon as** | immediately after | *Call as soon as you land.* |
| **once** | after (with "now that" feel) | *Once you start, don't stop.* |
| **whenever** | every time | *She visits whenever she can.* |
| **by the time** | before or at that point | *By the time I came, he left.* |
| **every time** | each occasion | *Every time I sneeze, I cough.* |
| **now that** | new situation (time+cause) | *Now that spring is here...* |
| **the moment** | instantly when | *The moment I saw her...* |
| **as long as** | for the duration | *Stay as long as you like.* |
| **no sooner...than** | immediate sequence (formal) | *No sooner said than done.* |
| **hardly...when** | immediate sequence (formal) | *Hardly had I arrived when...* |
