## ordinary version:

I still remember that was a fine day. After we had prepared for a picnic for a few days, we carried our parcels into the car trunk and drove into the country in an exciting mood. We found a shady spot under some trees. There was also a river near us so that we could wash our foods conveniently. We unpacked our hampers and took our picnic things out. 

Suddenly, after we had put up the picnic rent, my cousin found there were a few wasps flying over our rent. And then a great many of wasps began to attack us. We paid a great many of efforts to drive them away. While seeing a wasp nest in one of the trees near our rent, I shouted to everyone that we must leave. We seized our things in hurry and returned to our car pursued by wasps. Fortunately, none of us hurt and we drove away.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| that was a fine day | Missing "it" – "that was" is unclear; "it was" is correct | I still remember it was a fine day |
| prepared for a picnic | "Prepared for" means getting ready; but "prepared for a picnic" is fine, though "prepared" alone is more natural | had prepared for a picnic (acceptable) |
| carried our parcels into the car trunk | "Car trunk" is fine, but "boot" is British; also "into the trunk" is correct | carried our parcels into the car trunk |
| in an exciting mood | Correct but slightly unnatural – "excitedly" is simpler | excitedly |
| so that we could wash our foods conveniently | "Foods" is usually uncountable; "food" is better. Also "so that" implies purpose, which is fine | so we could wash our food conveniently |
| unpacked our hampers | Correct | (no change) |
| after we had put up the picnic rent | Typo – "rent" should be "tent" | after we had put up the picnic tent |
| flying over our rent | Typo – "rent" should be "tent" | flying over our tent |
| a great many of wasps | Wrong structure – "a great many" is followed directly by a noun (no "of") | a great many wasps |
| We paid a great many of efforts | Wrong expression – "paid efforts" is incorrect; use "made efforts" or "spent a lot of effort"; also "a great many" is for countable nouns, but "effort" is uncountable here | We made a great effort |
| While seeing a wasp nest | "While seeing" is unnatural – "When I saw" is better | When I saw a wasp nest |
| near our rent | Typo – "rent" should be "tent" | near our tent |
| we must leave | "Must" is correct but "had to" is better for past narration | we had to leave |
| We seized our things in hurry | Missing "a" – "in a hurry" is correct | We seized our things in a hurry |
| none of us hurt | Missing "was" – "none of us was hurt" or "none of us were hurt" | none of us was hurt |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

I still remember **it was** a fine day. After we had prepared for a picnic for a few days, we carried our parcels into the car trunk and drove into the country **excitedly**. We found a shady spot under some trees. There was also a river near us **so we could wash** our **food** conveniently. We unpacked our hampers and took our picnic things out. 

Suddenly, after we had put up the picnic **tent**, my cousin found there were a few wasps flying over our **tent**. And then **a great many** wasps began to attack us. We **made a great effort** to drive them away. **When I saw** a wasp nest in one of the trees near our **tent**, I shouted to everyone that we **had to** leave. We seized our things **in a hurry** and returned to our car, pursued by wasps. Fortunately, none of us **was hurt**, and we drove away.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

I still remember **it was a beautiful** day. After **preparing** for a picnic for a few days, we **loaded** our **bags** into the car trunk and drove **out** into the country **excitedly**. We found a shady spot under some trees **by a river**, **which was convenient for washing** our food. We **unpacked** the hampers and **spread out** our picnic things. 

Suddenly, **just** after we had put up the picnic tent, my cousin **noticed** a few wasps flying over **it**. **Soon**, a great many wasps began to attack us. We **struggled hard** to drive them away. **Then** I saw a wasp nest in one of the trees near our tent **and shouted** to everyone that we **had to get out**. We **grabbed** our things in a hurry and **ran back** to the car, **still** pursued by wasps. Fortunately, none of us **got stung**, and we **drove off**.
