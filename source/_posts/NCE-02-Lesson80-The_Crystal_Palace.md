## ordinary version:

Last weekend, I paid a visit to a modern exhibition held in the city gym. The exhibition whose topic was humanoid robot attracted a large crowds who lived nearby. I was also one of the visitors. I got up early in the morning and got to the gym by subway after breakfast. For there is a subway station near the gym, it’s easy for me to arrive. 

My first impression for the exhibition was how the humanoid robot industry could develop so fast. Humanoid robot, dog robot, things on display really caught our eyes. The exciting moment was two robots were dancing. The actions they showed were so hard that even human couldn’t do. I had a walk round the exhibition hall. The exhibits that I liked best was a dog robot which could run fast. I was tired at the end of the day, but the exhibition taught me a lot.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original                                        | Problem                                                      | Correction                                                   |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| whose topic was                                 | Awkward phrasing – "whose" is for possession, but "topic" not natural here | which focused on                                             |
| humanoid robot                                  | Singular when referring to the general field – use plural or "humanoid robots" | humanoid robots                                              |
| a large crowds                                  | "Crowds" is plural, but "a" is singular – mismatch           | a large crowd                                                |
| For there is a subway station                   | "For" as a conjunction is too formal/old-fashioned here; also tense mismatch ("is" vs past tense story) | Since there was a subway station                             |
| it's easy for me to arrive                      | "Arrive" needs a destination – "arrive there" or better "get there" | it was easy for me to get there                              |
| My first impression for                         | Wrong preposition – "impression of" is correct               | My first impression of                                       |
| could develop so fast                           | "Could" suggests possibility; "has developed" or "had developed" is better for observed progress | had developed so fast                                        |
| Humanoid robot, dog robot, things on display    | Sentence fragment – missing verb and awkward listing         | Humanoid robots, dog robots, and other things on display really caught my eye |
| really caught our eyes                          | "Caught our eyes" is not idiomatic – "caught my eye" (singular) is correct | caught my eye                                                |
| The exciting moment was two robots were dancing | Missing "that" after "was"                                   | The exciting moment was that two robots were dancing         |
| even human couldn't do                          | Missing "a" before "human" and missing object "them"         | even a human couldn't do them                                |
| had a walk round                                | "Round" is British English; "around" is more standard; also "took a walk" is more natural | took a walk around                                           |
| The exhibits that I liked best was              | Subject "exhibits" (plural) but verb "was" (singular) – mismatch | The exhibit that I liked best was (or change verb to "were") |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

Last weekend, I paid a visit to a modern exhibition held in the city gym. The exhibition **which focused on humanoid robots** attracted **a large crowd** who lived nearby. I was also one of the visitors. I got up early in the morning and got to the gym by subway after breakfast. **Since there was** a subway station near the gym, it **was** easy for me to **get there**. 

My first impression **of** the exhibition was how the humanoid robot industry **had developed** so fast. **Humanoid robots, dog robots, and other** things on display really caught **my eye**. The exciting moment was **that** two robots were dancing. The actions they showed were so hard that **even a human couldn't do them**. I **took a walk around** the exhibition hall. **The exhibit that** I liked best **was** a dog robot which could run fast. I was tired at the end of the day, but the exhibition taught me a lot.

My first impression **of** the exhibition was how **quickly** the humanoid robot industry **has developed**. **Humanoid robots, dog robots, and other displays** really caught **my eye**. The most exciting moment was **when** two robots were dancing. The **movements** they showed were so **difficult** that **even a human couldn't perform them**.
