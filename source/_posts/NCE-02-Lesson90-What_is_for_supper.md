## ordinary version:

Strange stories are often heard nowadays. I was visiting a hot oil rig recently. A worker told me that something strange happened when their diving team went down to work. While they were working at a depth of sixty feet, everything went well. But suddenly, one diver reported that a nasty bumped into him. As it was so dark under the sea, he could not see what the nasty was. On hearing this, the men on the rigs laughed and thought it mush be a monster. Today, the second team went down but came up immediately because they had encountered a huge cod eight feet length and a skate twelve feet across. At a depth of sixty feet without any light, they really looked like nasty. As it is, the divers refused to go down. After they had been bumped by the giant creatures again, the mangers had to promise to have the special cages made to protect them.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| hot oil rig | "Hot" is unusual – "offshore oil rig" or "oil rig" is correct; "hot" might mean active, but it's confusing | offshore oil rig |
| a nasty bumped into him | "Nasty" as a noun is incorrect here – "nasty" is an adjective. "Something nasty" or "a creature" is correct | a nasty creature bumped into him |
| he could not see what the nasty was | Same issue – "nasty" as a noun is wrong | what the creature was |
| the men on the rigs | "Rigs" is plural but the story refers to one rig – should be "rig" | on the rig |
| thought it mush be a monster | Typo – "mush" should be "must" | thought it must be a monster |
| Today | Tense issue – the story is past tense; "today" doesn't fit – use "The next day" or "Later" | The next day |
| eight feet length | Word order – should be "eight feet in length" or "eight-foot-long" | eight feet in length |
| a skate twelve feet across | Correct | (no change) |
| they really looked like nasty | Same issue – "nasty" as a noun – "they really looked like nasty creatures" | they really looked like nasty creatures |
| As it is | Wrong phrase – "As it is" means "in reality"; "As a result" or "Consequently" is better here | As a result |
| After they had been bumped by the giant creatures again | "They" refers to the divers, but the divers were bumped, not "they" – it's clear but could be rephrased. Also, "giant creatures" is clear but not introduced earlier – acceptable | After they had been bumped by the giant creatures again |
| the mangers | Typo – "mangers" should be "managers" | the managers |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

Strange stories are often heard nowadays. I was visiting **an offshore oil rig** recently. A worker told me that something strange happened when their diving team went down to work. While they were working at a depth of sixty feet, everything went well. But suddenly, one diver reported that **a nasty creature** bumped into him. As it was so dark under the sea, he could not see what **the creature** was. On hearing this, the men on **the rig** laughed and thought it **must** be a monster. **The next day**, the second team went down but came up immediately because they had encountered a huge cod **eight feet in length** and a skate twelve feet across. At a depth of sixty feet without any light, they really looked like **nasty creatures**. **As a result**, the divers refused to go down. After they had been bumped by the giant creatures again, **the managers** had to promise to have special cages made to protect them.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

Strange stories **are often heard these days**. I **recently visited** an offshore oil rig **where** a worker told me that something strange **had happened** when their diving team **went down** to work. While **working** at a depth of sixty feet, everything **went** well. But suddenly, one diver reported that **something** had bumped into him. **Since** it was so dark under the sea, he couldn't see what it was. **When** the men on the rig heard this, they laughed and thought it **must have been** a monster. **The following day**, **another** team went down but came up immediately because they **had run into** a huge cod **eight feet long** and a skate twelve feet across. At a depth of sixty feet with no light, **these giant fish** really looked like **monsters**. As a result, the divers refused to go down **again**. **But after** they **were** bumped by the giant creatures once more, the managers **promised** to have special cages made to protect them.
