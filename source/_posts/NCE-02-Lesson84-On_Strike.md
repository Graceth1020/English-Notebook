## ordinary version:

Unsurprisingly, the strike began in the follow week. Even though the students who volunteered to drive bus passed a special test, they drove badly. This caused a severe chaos to the whole city traffic. Because the buses were seldom on time, the public could not bear it anymore and started filling complaints with relevant authorities. On knowing this, the busmen were pleased that their goal had reached. However, complaints irritated the students. They threatened to go on strike. The authorities thought the students were just joking and did not pay much attention to their statements. To their surprise, students did so a few days later. They did not come to work on schedule which angered the busmen. After the government had signed a new agreement which reached their expectations, the busmen returned to work.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| in the follow week | Wrong word – "follow" as an adjective is incorrect; "following" is correct | in the following week |
| to drive bus | Missing article – "to drive the bus" or "to drive buses" | to drive buses |
| a severe chaos | "Chaos" is uncountable – cannot use "a" before it | severe chaos (remove "a") |
| to the whole city traffic | Preposition issue – "to" is odd; "for" or "in" is better, or restructure | for the entire city's traffic |
| started filling complaints | Wrong word – "filing complaints" (official term) is correct | started filing complaints |
| their goal had reached | Passive voice missing – should be "had been reached" | their goal had been reached |
| complaints irritated the students | Missing "the" before "complaints" (referring to previous complaints) | The complaints irritated the students |
| students did so | Ambiguous – "did so" refers to "went on strike," which is clear but slightly weak | the students did so (acceptable, but "went on strike" is clearer) |
| on schedule which angered | Missing comma before "which" to separate clauses | on schedule, which angered |
| After the government had signed | Past perfect unnecessary – simple past "signed" is fine | After the government signed |
| which reached their expectations | "Reached" is odd for expectations – "met their expectations" is correct | which met their expectations |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

Unsurprisingly, the strike began in **the following** week. Even though the students who volunteered to drive **buses** passed a special test, they drove badly. This caused **severe chaos** **for the entire city's traffic**. Because the buses were seldom on time, the public could not bear it anymore and started **filing** complaints with relevant authorities. On knowing this, the busmen were pleased that their goal **had been reached**. However, **the** complaints irritated the students. They threatened to go on strike. The authorities thought the students were just joking and did not pay much attention to their statements. To their surprise, **the students did so** a few days later. They did not come to work on schedule **,** which angered the busmen. After the government **signed** a new agreement **that met** their expectations, the busmen returned to work.
