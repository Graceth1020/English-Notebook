## ordinary version:

As we know, the Statue of Liberty, located at the entrance of New York harbor, is a symbol of Liberty for millions of people who goes through the harbor to make their homes in America. Last summer, I went to there to feel the liberty. It was early in the morning, we bought the tickets online and waited for ticket inspection. A large number of people had been queueing at the ticket gate. We got on board half an hour later. When we were sailing into N.Y. Harbor between Long Island and Staten Island, the Statue of Liberty appeared. Our fist impressions about the Status were how tall it was! My first glimpse of New York from Manhattan was the skycrapers towering into sky. The most striking buildings were the Empire State building and the United Nations building. Great ships were busy sailing into of out of the harbor. There were also many bridges with a log of cars, buses and trucks, and many people were waking on the pavements.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| who goes | Subject-verb agreement – "people" is plural, so "go" is correct | who go |
| Liberty | Lowercase "liberty" – not a proper noun here (unless referring to the statue name) | liberty |
| I went to there | "To there" is incorrect – "there" is an adverb, no "to" needed | I went there |
| to feel the liberty | "The liberty" is unnatural – "liberty" (abstract) doesn't need "the" | to feel liberty |
| It was early in the morning, we bought | Comma splice – two independent clauses joined by a comma | It was early in the morning. We bought |
| queueing | British spelling is "queueing" (acceptable) but "queuing" is more common | queuing |
| got on board | Correct for a ship | (no change) |
| sailing into N.Y. Harbor | "N.Y." is awkward in narration – "New York" is better | sailing into New York Harbor |
| Our fist impressions | Typo – "fist" should be "first" | Our first impressions |
| about the Status | Typo – "Status" should be "Statue" | about the Statue |
| how tall it was | Correct | (no change) |
| from Manhattan | Correct | (no change) |
| the skycrapers | Typo – "skycrapers" should be "skyscrapers" | the skyscrapers |
| towering into sky | Missing "the" – "into the sky" | towering into the sky |
| the Empire State building | "Building" should be capitalized as part of the name – "Empire State Building" | the Empire State Building |
| the United Nations building | "Building" should be capitalized as part of the name | the United Nations Building |
| sailing into of out of | Typo/word error – "into of" should be "into and out of" | sailing into and out of the harbor |
| a log of cars | Typo – "log" should be "lot" | a lot of cars |
| waking on the pavements | Typo – "waking" should be "walking" | walking on the pavements |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

As we know, the Statue of Liberty, located at the entrance of New York harbor, is a symbol of **liberty** for millions of people **who go** through the harbor to make their homes in America. Last summer, I went **there** to feel **liberty**. It was early in the morning **.** We bought the tickets online and waited for ticket inspection. A large number of people had been **queuing** at the ticket gate. We got on board half an hour later. When we were sailing into **New York** Harbor between Long Island and Staten Island, the Statue of Liberty appeared. Our **first** impressions **about the Statue** were how tall it was! My first glimpse of New York from Manhattan was **the skyscrapers** towering into **the sky**. The most striking buildings were **the Empire State Building** and **the United Nations Building**. Great ships were busy sailing into **and out of** the harbor. There were also many bridges with **a lot of** cars, buses and trucks, and many people were **walking** on the pavements.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

As we know, the Statue of Liberty, **standing** at the entrance of New York Harbor, **has long been** a symbol of liberty for millions of **immigrants** who **passed** through the harbor to make their homes in America. Last summer, I went there **to experience it** **myself**. It was early in the morning **when** we bought **our** tickets online and waited **for our turn**. A large **crowd** had been queuing at the ticket gate. **We boarded the ferry** half an hour later. As we sailed into New York Harbor between Long Island and Staten Island, the Statue of Liberty **came into view**. Our first impression **of** the Statue was **how immense** it was! My first glimpse of New York **from the water** was **of** skyscrapers towering into the sky. The most striking buildings were the Empire State Building and the United Nations Building. **Huge** ships were busy sailing into and out of the harbor. There were **also** many bridges **crowded** with cars, buses, and trucks, and **people** were walking **along** the pavements.
