# Countable Unit Nouns & Sentences
## 1. Substances & Materials
### 1.1 water
**Unit**: a glass / bottle / cup of water
- We need to drink plenty of water every day to keep healthy.
- If you feel tired after exercising, you should drink a glass of warm water immediately.

### 1.2 rice
**Unit**: a bowl / bag of rice
- People in southern China mainly eat rice as their staple food.
- By the time my parents got home, I had already cooked a bowl of steamed rice for dinner.

### 1.3 bread
**Unit**: a piece / slice / loaf of bread
- My mother usually bakes fresh bread on weekends.
- While I was preparing breakfast, I cut a slice of bread and spread jam on it.

### 1.4 milk
**Unit**: a glass / carton / bottle of milk
- A glass of warm milk helps you fall asleep quickly.
- The little boy has drunk a whole carton of milk since he finished his morning exercises.

### 1.5 wood
**Unit**: a piece / block of wood
- This old table is made of solid wood.
- The carpenter will use a heavy block of wood to make a brand-new bookshelf tomorrow.

### 1.6 paper
**Unit**: a piece / sheet of paper
- Please don’t waste paper when taking notes.
- Before the meeting started, everyone was asked to write their ideas on a sheet of white paper.

### 1.7 glass
**Unit**: a piece of glass
- The window glass broke in the strong wind last night.
- Be careful not to step on a sharp piece of broken glass that lies on the ground.

### 1.8 sand
**Unit**: a handful of sand
- Children love playing with sand on the beach.
- As the tide rose, the little girl scattered a handful of sand into the gentle sea wind.

### 1.9 soil
**Unit**: a handful / pile of soil
- Rich soil helps plants grow better.
- If you want the flower to grow well, you have to add a pile of fertile soil into the flower pot first.

### 1.10 oil
**Unit**: a drop / bottle of oil
- This dish tastes better with a little cooking oil.
- The chef said that just one drop of special oil can greatly improve the flavor of the soup.

## 2. Abstract Concepts
### 2.1 time
**Unit**: a period of time
- I don’t have enough time to finish the report today.
- We have spent a long period of time researching and discussing this difficult project.

### 2.2 money
**Unit**: a sum of money
- Happiness cannot be bought with money.
- The kind businessman donated a large sum of money to help children in poor mountain areas.

### 2.3 advice
**Unit**: a piece of advice
- My teacher gave me useful advice on English learning.
- What you have told me is the most valuable piece of advice that I have ever received.

### 2.4 information
**Unit**: a piece / bit of information
- You can find lots of useful information online.
- Nobody knew that he had hidden an important piece of information from all of us.

### 2.5 news
**Unit**: a piece of news
- Have you heard the latest news about the sports meeting?
- When we were chatting yesterday, she shared a surprising piece of news with the whole class.

### 2.6 knowledge
**Unit**: a bit / body of knowledge
- We gain knowledge by reading and practicing.
- The senior professor owns a huge body of professional knowledge in traditional medicine.

### 2.7 fun
**Unit**: a bit of fun
- We had great fun at the party last night.
- Even though we were busy, we still found a bit of fun during the long journey.

### 2.8 luck
**Unit**: a bit of luck
- Good luck with your final exam!
- You will pass the interview as long as you stay calm and have a little bit of luck.

### 2.9 courage
**Unit**: a great deal of courage
- It takes great courage to face difficulties bravely.
- It took her a great deal of courage to admit her mistakes in front of all colleagues.

### 2.10 patience
**Unit**: a great deal of patience
- Teaching young kids requires much patience.
- Taking care of elderly patients needs a great deal of patience and sincere care.

### 2.11 progress
**Unit**: a great deal of progress
- She has made great progress in spoken English.
- Under the teacher’s guidance, these students have made a great deal of progress recently.

### 2.12 health
**Unit**: a state of health
- Regular exercise is good for your health.
- After keeping healthy lifestyles for years, he is always in an excellent state of health.

### 2.13 peace
**Unit**: a state of peace
- Everyone hopes to live in peace forever.
- This remote village has remained in a peaceful state of peace for hundreds of years.

### 2.14 love
**Unit**: a great deal of love
- A family is full of warmth and love.
- Parents have devoted a great deal of love to their children throughout their lives.

### 2.15 experience
**Unit**: a wealth of experience / a bit of experience
- Traveling broadens our horizons and enriches our life experience.
- After working in this field for decades, he has gained a wealth of practical work experience.

### 2.16 difference
**Unit**: a world of difference / a slight difference
- You can hardly notice the difference between the two similar designs.
- There is a world of difference between living in a big city and living in the countryside.

## 3. Feelings & Emotions
### 3.1 happiness
**Unit**: a feeling of happiness
- True happiness comes from simple daily life.
- When she reunited with her family, a warm feeling of happiness spread across her face.

### 3.2 sadness
**Unit**: a feeling of sadness
- Her eyes were full of sadness after hearing the bad news.
- A sudden feeling of sadness came over me when I thought of my faraway hometown.

### 3.3 anger
**Unit**: a burst of anger
- He tried to control his anger and stay calm.
- He walked out of the room right after a sudden burst of anger.

### 3.4 fear
**Unit**: a feeling of fear
- The little girl felt no fear in her mother’s arms.
- A strange feeling of fear will appear if you stay alone in the dark for too long.

## 4. Weather & Nature
### 4.1 rain
**Unit**: a drop of rain / a shower of rain
- Heavy rain stopped us from going out for a picnic.
- The first drop of rain had fallen before we managed to get back home.

### 4.2 snow
**Unit**: a flake of snow
- The ground is covered with white snow in winter.
- As we looked up, a soft white flake of snow slowly landed on my thick coat.

### 4.3 wind
**Unit**: a gust of wind
- A strong wind blew all the leaves off the trees.
- A fierce gust of wind blew the wooden door open while we were having dinner.

### 4.4 fog
**Unit**: a patch of fog
- Thick fog made driving dangerous this morning.
- A thick patch of fog has covered the highway, so all vehicles have to slow down.

### 4.5 light
**Unit**: a ray of light
- The sunlight streamed through the window.
- When night fell, a thin ray of light shone through the narrow crack of the wall.

## 5. Food & Drinks
### 5.1 juice
**Unit**: a glass / bottle of juice
- I prefer fresh fruit juice to soda.
- After finishing the heavy work, she poured herself a full glass of fresh orange juice.

### 5.2 coffee
**Unit**: a cup of coffee
- Many adults drink coffee to stay awake in the daytime.
- If you stay up late working, a cup of black coffee can help you keep energetic.

### 5.3 tea
**Unit**: a cup of tea
- Drinking tea is a traditional habit in China.
- Every time my grandfather finishes his work, he will make a cup of hot tea to relax.

### 5.4 cheese
**Unit**: a piece / slice of cheese
- She put some cheese on the bread before eating.
- You will taste better flavor if you add a thin slice of cheese on the hamburger.

### 5.5 yogurt
**Unit**: a cup / pot of yogurt
- Yogurt is good for our stomach.
- I have kept the habit of eating a cup of plain yogurt before going to bed every night.

## 6. Other Common Uncountable Nouns
### 6.1 traffic
**Unit**: a stream of traffic
- There is always heavy traffic during rush hours.
- A steady stream of traffic moves slowly along the main road every morning.

### 6.2 luggage / baggage
**Unit**: a piece of luggage / baggage
- All my luggage is already packed for the trip.
- Among all the bags, I can only carry one piece of luggage by myself.

### 6.3 furniture
**Unit**: a piece of furniture
- This set of furniture matches the room perfectly.
- This antique cabinet is the most precious piece of furniture in the old house.

### 6.4 equipment
**Unit**: a piece of equipment
- The lab has new scientific equipment now.
- The school has just bought a professional piece of equipment for the physics lab.

### 6.5 homework
**Unit**: a bit of homework
- The students have too much homework every day.
- Even though it was late, I still had a bit of homework left to complete.

### 6.6 housework
**Unit**: a bit of housework
- I help my parents do housework after school.
- I spent my whole morning doing a bit of housework and tidying up every room.

### 6.7 work
**Unit**: a piece of work
- Hard work will bring you good results.
- What he has finished is indeed a challenging and delicate piece of work.
