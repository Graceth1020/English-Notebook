## Mistakes Identified:

| Original | Problem | Correction |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| wlas | Typo – "wlas" should be "was" | was |
| a dark night, a prisoner | Comma splice – two independent clauses joined only by a comma | a dark night. A prisoner |
| killed his guard | Tense issue – killing happened before dragging, so past perfect is better | had killed his guard |
| dragged him | "Him" refers to the guard – clear, but ensure logical flow (the prisoner first killed, then dragged the same guard) | (no change, but context clear) |
| changed Into his clothes | "Into" should not be capitalized; also "changed into" is correct | changed into his clothes |
| As he was dressed as a guard | Grammatically correct – "dressed as a guard" means wearing guard's uniform | (no change) |
| without any concerns | Correct – "without any concerns" or "without concern" both fine | (no change) |
| He fellow a group | Wrong word – "fellow" is a noun (a person), but verb needed – "followed" | He followed a group |
| search the missing prisoner and the prisoner took part | Missing comma before "and" to separate clauses | search the missing prisoner, and the prisoner took part |
| was said to be seen | Awkward passive combination – "was said to have been seen" or "was reportedly seen" | was reportedly seen |
| On arriving the countryside | Missing preposition – "on arriving at" or "upon arriving in" | On arriving at the countryside (or "Upon arriving in the countryside") |
| caught this once in the lifetime opportunity | Wrong idiom – correct phrase is "once-in-a-lifetime opportunity" | caught this once-in-a-lifetime opportunity |

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

It **was** a dark night **. A** prisoner of war who **had killed** his guard dragged him into the bushes and changed **into** his clothes. As he was dressed as a guard, he was still in the camp without any concerns. He **followed** a group of guards, who were marching up and down in front of the camp. The group leader received a command to search **for** the missing prisoner **,** and the prisoner took part in the search.

A lorry was waiting outside the camp. The prisoner got on the lorry together with the guards. They drove into the countryside where the prisoner was **reportedly seen**. **Upon arriving at** the countryside, the guards went into the forest and began their search. The prisoner caught **this once-in-a-lifetime** opportunity and escaped.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

It **was** a dark night **when** a prisoner of war who **had killed** his guard dragged **the body** into the bushes and changed into **the guard's** clothes. **Now** dressed as a guard, he **remained** in the camp without any concerns. He **followed** a group of guards **marching** up and down in front of the camp. The group leader received **orders** to search **for** the missing prisoner **,** and **the escaped prisoner** took part in the search.

A lorry was waiting outside the camp. The prisoner got on the lorry together with **the other** guards. They drove into the countryside where the prisoner **was believed to have been seen**. **Upon reaching** the countryside, the guards went into the forest and began their search. The prisoner **seized** this **once-in-a-lifetime** opportunity and escaped.
