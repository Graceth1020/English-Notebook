## ordinary version:

The harvest is over, the exciting macaroni-eating contest is taking place. The appearance of six competitors catches our attention. They are all overweight except for one young man who is thin but strong. The host introduced their past achievements. After that, a huge quantity of macaroni is prepared, weighed and served. Every competitor sits in front of a table, ready for the contest. Not only quantity but also speed is important. The competitors have been eating for three hours. In the end, only one man remains. Yes, it's the young man – thinner than anyone else. To our surprise, he asks for more. It seems he hasn't eaten for a long time.

## Mistakes Identified:

| Original                                                     | Problem                                                      | Correction                                                   |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| The harvest is over, the exciting macaroni-eating contest is taking place | Comma splice – two independent clauses joined only by a comma | The harvest is over, **and** the exciting macaroni-eating contest is taking place |
| The host introduced                                          | Tense shift – story is in present tense ("is taking place," "catches"), but "introduced" is past | The host **introduces**                                      |
| their past achievements                                      | "Their" refers to competitors – fine, but slight ambiguity; "their" is acceptable | (no change needed)                                           |
| Not only quantity but also speed is important                | Grammatically correct, but "but also" could be clearer with "but speed is also important" – not a mistake | (no change needed)                                           |
| In the end, only one man remains                             | Correct                                                      | (no change needed)                                           |
| thinner than anyone else                                     | Correct – "anyone else" excludes himself                     | (no change needed)                                           |
| It seems he hasn't eaten for a long time                     | Correct                                                      | (no change needed)                                           |

**Note:** Your paragraph is actually very well written. The only clear errors are the **comma splice** in the first sentence and the **tense shift** from present to past. All other grammar is correct.

## Corrected Version (changes in bold):

The harvest is over, **and** the exciting macaroni-eating contest is taking place. The appearance of six competitors catches our attention. They are all overweight except for one young man who is thin but strong. The host **introduces** their past achievements. After that, a huge quantity of macaroni is prepared, weighed and served. Every competitor sits in front of a table, ready for the contest. Not only quantity but also speed is important. The competitors have been eating for three hours. In the end, only one man remains. Yes, it's the young man – thinner than anyone else. To our surprise, he asks for more. It seems he hasn't eaten for a long time.

## Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

The harvest is over, **and now** the exciting macaroni-eating contest **is about to begin**. The appearance of six competitors catches our attention. They are all overweight **except** for one young man who is thin but strong. The host **introduces** their past achievements. **Then**, a huge quantity of macaroni is prepared, weighed, and served. **Each** competitor sits **at** a table, ready for the contest. Not only quantity but **speed** is also important. The competitors **have been eating** for three hours. In the end, only one man remains. Yes, it's the young man – thinner than **all the others**. To our surprise, he asks for more. It seems he **hadn't** eaten for a long time.
